---

## 2. Grammar & Sentences

**Describing Current Emotional States (Verbs):** For verbs like おこる (to get angry), use the 〜ています form to describe a currently ongoing feeling or state.

- **Grammar Rule:** `[Verb て-form]` + います
- **Structure:** `[Subject]` は `[Verb て-form]` います
- **Key Phrase:** おこっています。— "(I am / someone is) angry."

**Time Context:** Combine 今 (いま) with state-based verbs/adjectives to clarify the present moment.

---

## 3. Learning Context

- **Verb vs. Adjective:** Adjectives (e.g. うれしい) describe feelings directly; verbs (e.g. おこる) need the continuous form 〜ています to express a current state of mind.
- **U-Verb Conjugation:** おこる is a U-verb — its て-form conjugates to おこって.
