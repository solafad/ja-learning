---

## 2. Grammar & Sentences

**Negative i-Adjective Form:** To say "not [adjective]," change the final 〜い to 〜くない.

- **Structure:** `[Adjective base]` + くないです
- **Example:** さびしい → さびしくないです *(I am not lonely.)*

**Emphasizing Negation:** Use ぜんぜん (zenzen) before a negative verb or adjective to mean "not at all."

- **Example:** ぜんぜんさびしくないです。*(I'm not lonely at all.)*

**Describing Situations:** `[Reason/Context]` + `[Feeling]`

- **Example:** 一人で晩ご飯を食べるのはさびしいです。*(Eating dinner alone is lonely.)*

---

## 3. Learning Context

- **Practical Application:** Practiced responding to "How do you feel?" (どんな気持ちですか) in various social and solitary scenarios.
- **Contextual Nuance:** さびしい is used when someone lacks company or is busy alone; はずかしい applies to social slips or being the center of attention.
- **Achievement:** Successfully identified the correct emotional responses for Toshiki's situation (eating alone while busy).
