## 2. Grammar & Sentences

### は — Topic Marker (main message comes *after* は)

は introduces what you are talking about. The important information — the predicate — follows は.

- 私は日本人です。*(Watashi wa nihonjin desu.)* — I am Japanese.
- カーラさんはフランス人です。*(Kaara-san wa furansujin desu.)* — Carla is French.
- ナルトはにんじゃです。*(Naruto wa ninja desu.)* — Naruto is a ninja.
- このくつは高いです。*(Kono kutsu wa takai desu.)* — These shoes are expensive.
- J-POPは日本の音楽です。*(J-POP wa nihon no ongaku desu.)* — J-pop is Japanese music.

**は for contrast** — when comparing two things, both subjects take は:

- 日本の夏は暑いです。でも、冬は寒いです。*(Nihon no natsu wa atsui desu. Demo, fuyu wa samui desu.)* — Summer in Japan is hot. But winter is cold.
- 日本語は話せます。でも漢字は書けません。*(Nihongo wa hanasemasu. Demo kanji wa kakemasen.)* — I can speak Japanese. But I can't write kanji.
- 肉は好きです。さかなはきらいです。*(Niku wa suki desu. Sakana wa kirai desu.)* — I like meat. I hate fish.

### が — New / Important Information (main info comes *before* が)

が highlights the subject as the key new piece of information.

- 友だちが来ます。*(Tomodachi ga kimasu.)* — A **friend** is coming.
- 雨がふります。*(Ame ga furimasu.)* — It will rain.
- 北海道でゆきがふっています。*(Hokkaido de yuki ga futte imasu.)* — It is snowing in Hokkaido.
- 本があります。*(Hon ga arimasu.)* — There is a book.
- 頭がいたいんです。*(Atama ga itain desu.)* — I have a headache.

### Combined は … が Pattern

When the topic is set with は and a preference or ability verb follows, the object of that verb takes が:

- みきおさんは音楽が好きです。*(Mikio-san wa ongaku ga suki desu.)* — Mikio likes music.
- ことみさんは空手がとくいです。*(Kotomi-san wa karate ga tokui desu.)* — Kotomi is good at karate.

## 3. Learning Context

- **は rule:** The end of the sentence is the main point — whatever comes after は is what you want to convey.
- **が rule:** The beginning of the sentence is the main point — が marks *new* or *highlighted* information, not a contrasting topic.
- **Fixed が words:** 好きです、上手です、とくいです、できます always take が — even inside a は…が sentence.
