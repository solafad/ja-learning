## 2. Grammar & Sentences

**Adverbial "More and More":** Use どんどん to describe a continuous or rapid progression.

**Converting Adjectives to Adverbs:**

- **Na-adjectives:** Add に after the adjective to turn it into an adverb.
- **Example:** 好き → 好きに *(suki → suki ni)*

**Key Phrase:** どんどん好きになります。*(Dondon suki ni narimasu.)* — "I'm coming to like it more and more."

**Progressive Improvement:** Combining practice with どんどん.

- **Example:** 練習すれば、どんどん上手になります。*(Renshuu sureba, dondon jouzu ni narimasu.)* — "If you practice, you will steadily get better."

---

## 3. Learning Context

- **Word Nuance:** どんどん emphasizes the *speed and rhythm* of change — very common when discussing language learning or developing hobbies.
- **Grammar Mechanic:** When na-adjectives describe a change (like "becoming" something), the に particle is required.
