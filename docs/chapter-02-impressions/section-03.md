# Onomatopoeia for feelings and actions

## 1. Vocabulary List

| Japanese       | Romaji       | English                                    |
|----------------|--------------|--------------------------------------------|
| どきどきする    | dokidoki suru | To have a pounding heart (nervous/excited) |
| いらいらする    | iraira suru  | To be irritated / annoyed                  |
| にこにこする    | nikoniko suru | To smile / be smiley                      |
| ぺらぺら       | perapera     | Fluent (speaking)                          |
| ばっちり       | bacchiri     | Perfect / spot on                          |
| どんどん       | dondon       | More and more / steadily                   |

---

## 2. Grammar & Sentences

**Onomatopoeic Verbs:** Many Japanese sound-effect words (擬声語 / 擬態語) become verbs by adding 〜する.

- **Example:** どきどきします。*(My heart is pounding.)*

**Adverbial Use:** Words like ぺらぺら and ばっちり describe *how* an action is performed.

- 彼は日本語がぺらぺらです。*(Kare wa nihongo ga perapera desu.)* — "He is fluent in Japanese."
- 準備はばっちりです。*(Junbi wa bacchiri desu.)* — "The preparations are perfect."

---

## 3. Learning Context

- **Sound Origins:** どんどん (more and more) stems from the sound of taiko drums — a steady, rhythmic progression.
- **Physical vs. Emotional:** どきどき can represent both a racing heartbeat and the emotional feeling of nerves or excitement ("butterflies").
- **Usage Nuance:** にこにこ describes a constant, pleasant manner of smiling — often used to characterize someone's friendly demeanor.
