## 2. Kanji Breakdown

### 重 — Heavy / Weight

| Form       | Reading | Meaning       |
|------------|---------|---------------|
| 重い        | おもい   | Heavy (adj)   |
| 重さ        | おもさ   | Weight (noun) |
| 体重        | たいじゅう | Body weight  |

The 重 kanji carries two meaning layers: the *quality* of being heavy (重い) and the *quantity* of weight (重さ, 体重).

### 身 — Person / Body

| Form  | Reading | Meaning     |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| 身長   | しんちょう | Body height |

身 means "person" or "body" as a kanji component. Combined with 長 (long/length → ちょう) it forms 身長 = body height.

### 泳 — Swim

| Form  | Reading | Meaning  |
|-------|---------|----------|
| 泳ぐ   | およぐ   | To swim  |
| 水泳   | すいえい  | Swimming |

泳 appears in 水泳 (すいえい) — 水 means water. The verb form is 泳ぐ（およぐ）.

### 走 — Run

| Form  | Reading | Meaning |
|-------|---------|---------|
| 走る   | はしる   | To run  |

走 is the kanji for はしる. The compound 走る is a common everyday verb.

### 肉 — Meat

| Form  | Reading | Meaning     |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| 肉     | にく     | Meat        |
| 肉屋   | にくや   | Butcher's   |

肉（にく）is also a kanji component meaning "flesh/body" — it appears in 体重 via the 体 kanji (からだ / たい).

## 3. Grammar & Sentences

- 重い(おもい) / 重さ(おもさ) / 体重(たいじゅう) *(omoi / omosa / taijuu)* — heavy / weight / body weight
- 身長と体重を教えてください。*(Shinchou to taijuu wo oshiete kudasai.)* — Please tell me your height and weight.
- 走ることと泳ぐことが好きです。*(Hashiru koto to oyogu koto ga suki desu.)* — I like running and swimming.
- 泳ぐ(およぐ) / 水泳(すいえい) *(oyogu / suiei)* — to swim / swimming

---

## 4. Learning Context

- **重い vs 重さ:** 重い is an i-adjective ("it is heavy"); 重さ is the noun form ("the weight of something"). 体重 uses the on-reading じゅう in a compound.
- **身 vs 体:** Both relate to the body, but 身 (しん) in 身長 focuses on the person as a whole, while 体 (たい/からだ) in 体重 refers to the physical body.
- **泳ぐ → 水泳:** The kanji 泳 uses えい in compounds. The verb form is entirely hiragana-rooted: およぐ.
- **肉屋 word-building:** 屋（や）added to a noun creates the word for the shop that sells that thing — 肉屋 (butcher's), 本屋 (bookshop), etc.
- **Kanji review exercise:** 幸せ 悲しい 重い — the lesson revisits earlier kanji (幸、悲) alongside the new 重, reinforcing multi-chapter kanji memory.
