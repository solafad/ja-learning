## 2. Kanji Pattern — 着

The kanji 着（き・ちゃく）means "to wear / clothing." It combines with directional prefixes to make clothing words:

| Japanese | Reading | Meaning | Pattern |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|
| 上着 | うわぎ | Coat / jacket | 上（above）+ 着 = outer clothing |
| 下着 | したぎ | Underwear | 下（below）+ 着 = under clothing |
| 水着 | みずぎ | Swimwear | 水（water）+ 着 = water clothing |

## 3. Grammar & Sentences

**Verb ない form ＋ でください — Please don't do ~**

To politely ask someone not to do something:

- **Structure:** `Verb (ない form, drop い)` + `ないでください`
- **Example:** 歯ブラシを忘れないでください。*(Haburashi wo wasurenaide kudasai.)* — "Please don't forget (to bring) a toothbrush."
- **Example:** 充電器を忘れないでください。*(Juudenki wo wasurenaide kudasai.)* — "Please do not forget (to bring) a charger."

**て form ＋ おく — Doing something in advance (revision)**

- **Example:** かばんに下着とはブラシとじゅう電きを入れておきました。*(Kaban ni shitagi to haburashi to juudenki wo irete okimashita.)* — "I put some underwear, a toothbrush and a charger in my bag (in advance)."
- **Example:** 旅行の前に、新しいせっけんを買っておきました。*(Ryokoo no mae ni, atarashii sekken wo katte okimashita.)* — "Before the trip, I bought some new soap (in advance)."

**Passive — 作られる (to be made from):**

- **Example:** このシャンプーとせっけんは花から作られます。*(Kono shanpuu to sekken wa hana kara tsukuraremasu.)* — "This shampoo and soap are made from flowers."
- **Note:** The wrong choices for blanks were タオル (towel) and けしゴム (rubber).
