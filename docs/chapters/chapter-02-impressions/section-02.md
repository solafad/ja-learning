# Describing impressions

## 1. Vocabulary List

| Japanese          | Romaji            | English                      |
|-------------------|-------------------|------------------------------|
| うれしそうです     | ureshi sou desu   | Looks glad / happy           |
| くやしそうです     | kuyashi sou desu  | Looks frustrated             |
| さびしそうです     | sabishi sou desu  | Looks lonely                 |
| さびしそうでした   | sabishi sou deshita | Looked lonely (past)       |

---

## 2. Grammar & Sentences

**Expressing Impressions (〜そう):** Used to describe how someone else appears to be feeling based on their outward appearance.

**Conjugation Rule (i-adjectives):** Remove the final 〜い and add 〜そうです.

- うれしい → うれしそうです *(Looks glad)*
- さびしい → さびしそうです *(Looks lonely)*

**Past Tense Impressions:** Change です to でした.

- **Example:** れんさんは今日さびしそうでした。*(Ren-san wa kyou sabishi sou deshita.)* — "Ren looked lonely today."

---

## 3. Learning Context

- **Observation vs. Direct Feeling:** Unlike previous lessons, you are now describing the feelings of *others* using visual cues rather than your own internal state.
- **Nuance:** In Japanese, you generally avoid saying "彼は悲しいです" (He is sad) because you cannot truly know someone else's heart — 〜そう is used instead to mean "He *looks* sad."
