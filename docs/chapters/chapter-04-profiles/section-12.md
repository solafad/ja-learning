## 3. Grammar & Sentences

**Passive form 〜られました / 〜られた — Was done (by someone/something):**

- **Structure:** `Verb (passive)` + `ました / た`
- **Example:** 一番古いまんがは1200年くらいに書かれました。*(Ichiban furui manga wa 1200-nen kurai ni kakaremashita.)* — "The oldest manga was written around 1200."
- **Example:** カメラつきのけいたい電話は日本で発明されました。*(Kamera-tsuki no keitai denwa wa Nihon de hatsumei saremashita.)* — "Mobile phones with cameras were invented in Japan."
- **Example:** この家はお父さんが生まれる前に建てられた。*(Kono ie wa otōsan ga umareru mae ni taterareta.)* — "This house was built before my father was born."

**〜そうです — Looks like / Seems (impressions):**

When sharing impressions of something we observe, attach そうです to an adjective stem.

- **Structure:** `i-adjective stem` + `そうです`
- **Example:** 友だちがいなくてさびしそうです。*(Tomodachi ga inakute sabishisoo desu.)* — "He doesn't have friends and he looks lonely."
- **Example:** あきとくんに話すとき、はずかしそう。*(Akito-kun ni hanasu toki, hazukashisoo.)* — "When you talk to Akito, you look embarrassed."

**〜ことです — Nominalisation (hobby / what something is):**

- **Structure:** `Verb (dictionary form)` + `ことです`
- **Example:** 趣味は本を読むことです。*(Shumi wa hon wo yomu koto desu.)* — "My hobby is reading books."

**〜てください — Please do ~:**

- **Structure:** `Verb (て form)` + `ください`
- **Example:** 日本語で話してください。*(Nihongo de hanashite kudasai.)* — "Please talk in Japanese."
