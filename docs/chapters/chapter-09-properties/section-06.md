## 2. Grammar & Sentences

**危ない vs 危険:** Both mean "dangerous" but behave differently.

- **危ない** is an **i-adjective** — modifies nouns directly: 危ない場所 *(abunai basho)* — "dangerous place"
- **危険** is a **noun / na-adjective** — requires な before a noun: 危険な場所 *(kiken na basho)* — "dangerous place"

---

**以上 / 以下 — "Or more / Or less":**

- **Structure:** `[Number]` + 以上 / 以下
- **Example (以下):** 千円以下。*(Sen en ika.)* — "1,000 yen or less."
- **Example (以上/以下 pair):** 以上（いじょう）/ 以下（いか）— "or more / or less"

---

**だから — "Because / So":**

Connects a reason to a result in plain/casual speech.

- **Structure:** `[Reason]` + だから + `[Result]`
- **Key Phrase:** 危険だから入らないでください。*(Kiken dakara hairanaide kudasai.)* — "Because it's dangerous, please do not enter."

---

**探す — "To look for":**

- **Key Phrase:** アパートを探す。*(Apāto wo sagasu.)* — "I'll look for an apartment."
- **Extended Example:** 家ちんが十万円以下のマンションを探しています。*(Yachin ga jūman en ika no manshon wo sagashite imasu.)* — "I'm looking for an apartment with a rent of 100,000 yen or less."

---

## 3. Learning Context

- **Kanji radicals:**
  - 危 — radical 巳 (seal); left part resembles a cliff
  - 険 — radical 阝 (mound); shared with 降
  - 全 — radical 人/へ (people/roof); shared with 会 and 今
  - 以 — radical 人 (people); shared with 人
  - 探 — radical 扌 (hand); shared with 持
- **Topic:** Describing safety and searching for property with numeric limits
- **Key contrast:** 危ない (i-adj) vs 危険 (noun/na-adj) — same meaning, different grammar behaviour
