## 2. Grammar & Sentences

**代（だい） — Utility Bill Suffix:**

Add 代 after a utility name to say its bill/charge.

- 電話 → 電話代 *(denwadai)* — phone bill
- 電気 → 電気代 *(denkidai)* — electricity bill
- ガス → ガス代 *(gasudai)* — gas bill
- 水道 → 水道代 *(suidōdai)* — water bill

---

**かかる — "To cost / To take":**

Used for both money and time costs. When no specific amount is stated, use が before かかります.

- **Structure (unspecified):** `[Subject]` + が + かかります
- **Structure (specified):** `[Subject]` + は + `[Amount]` + かかります
- **Example 1:** 引っこしはお金がかかります。*(Hikkoshi wa okane ga kakarimasu.)* — "Moving costs money."
- **Example 2:** 家にガスがないので、ガスだいはかかりません。*(Ie ni gasu ga nai node, gasudai wa kakarimasen.)* — "Because there is no gas at home, I do not pay a gas bill."
- **Example 3:** 料理が好きなので、電気だいとガスだいがかかります。*(Ryōri ga suki na node, denkidai to gasudai ga kakarimasu.)* — "Because I like cooking, I spend on electricity and gas."

---

**から — "Because" (casual reason connector):**

- **Structure:** `[Reason clause]` + から + `[Result clause]`
- **Key Phrase:** 夏はエアコンを使うから、電気だいが高くなる。*(Natsu wa eakon wo tsukau kara, denkidai ga taka ku naru.)* — "Because I use an air conditioner during the summer, the electricity bill becomes expensive."

> **Note:** 高くなる means "to become expensive". から is more casual than ので for explaining reasons.

---

**Culture note — Consumption tax (消費税):**

- Standard rate: **10%** on products and services
- Reduced rate: **8%** on food and drinks consumed at home
- 税込み (ぜいこみ) = displayed price **with** consumption tax included
- **Example:** しょうひぜいがかかります。*(Shōhizei ga kakarimasu.)* — "Consumption tax will be charged."

---

**Key Sentences:**

- 明日家ちんをはらいます。*(Ashita yachin wo haraimasu.)* — "I'll pay the rent tomorrow."
- 今月の電気だいは八千円でした。*(Kongetsu no denkidai wa hassen en deshita.)* — "The electricity bill for this month was 8,000 yen."
- 毎月一日に水道だいをはらいます。*(Maitsuki tsuitachi ni suidōdai wo haraimasu.)* — "I pay the water bill on the first of every month."
- 日本からアメリカまでの電話だいはいくらですか。*(Nihon kara Amerika made no denwadai wa ikura desu ka.)* — "How much is a phone call from Japan to the US?"

---

## 3. Learning Context

- **Topic:** Utility bills, moving costs, and tax in Japan
- **Key pattern:** `[Utility]` + 代 to form bill names; かかる for costs without specifying amount
- **から vs ので:** both mean "because"; から is more casual/direct, ので is softer and more polite
- **Real-world use:** Reading utility bills, discussing monthly living expenses
