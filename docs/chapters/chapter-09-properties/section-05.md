## 2. Grammar & Sentences

### 以下（いか） — "Or less / Below"

Used to set an **upper limit** on a number — most commonly for rent, price, or distance.

- **Structure:** `[Number]` + 以下
- **Example:** 家賃が六万円以下のアパートに住みたいです。*(Yachin ga rokuman en ika no apāto ni sumitai desu.)* — "I want to live in an apartment with rent of 60,000 yen or less."

---

### つきの — "With (a feature)"

Used to describe a property that **comes with** a specific feature or amenity.

- **Structure:** `[Feature noun]` + つきの + `[property noun]`
- **Example:** 駐車場つきのアパートに住んでいます。*(Chūshajō tsuki no apāto ni sunde imasu.)* — "I live in an apartment with a car park."

> **Note:** **つきの** is used to add an extra feature to a house or property listing.

---

### ので — "Because / Since" (reason connector)

Connects a reason to a result in a natural, polite way.

- **Structure:** `[Reason clause]` + ので + `[Result clause]`
- **Example:** コンビニがとなりにあるので便利ですね。*(Konbini ga tonari ni aru node benri desu ne.)* — "You have a convenience store next door and it's convenient, isn't it?"

---

### どちらのほうが好きですか — "Which do you like more?"

Used to ask someone to **compare two options** and choose a preference.

- **Key Phrase:** どちらのほうが好きですか。*(Dochira no hō ga suki desu ka.)* — "Which do you like more?"

> **Note:** This asks for a **preference between two things** — not whether you like both.

---

## 3. Learning Context

- **Topic:** Specifying numeric limits and property features when apartment hunting
- **Key Structures:** 以下 for upper limits, つきの for included amenities, ので for reasons
- **Real-world use:** Reading and discussing property listings; asking about neighbourhood safety
