## 2. Grammar & Sentences

### Verb noun formation — dropping ます

When you drop **ます** from a verb (masu-form), it becomes a **noun**.

| Verb (masu-form) | Meaning | Noun | Meaning |
|------------------|---------|------|---------|
| 引っこします | to move | 引っこし | move |
| 手伝います | to help | 手伝い | help |

> **Usage:** Add **屋** to a noun to mean a company or shop. 引っこし屋 = moving company.

---

### から — "Because" (reason)

- **Structure:** `[Reason clause]` + から、`[Result clause]`
- **Key Phrase:** トラックが来たから、はこを持って行きます。*(Torakku ga kita kara, hako o motte ikimasu.)* — "Because the truck has arrived, we'll take out the boxes."

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| 友だちにたのみます。 | Tomodachi ni tanomimasu. | I rely on my friends. |
| 友だちに引っこしの手伝いをたのみます。 | Tomodachi ni hikkoshi no tetsudai o tanomimasu. | I asked my friends to help me move. |
| 大きい物は引っこし屋にたのみます。 | Ōkii mono wa hikkoshi-ya ni tanomimasu. | For the large items, I'll rely on the moving company. |
| しょうたさんは引っこし屋でアルバイトをします。 | Shōta-san wa hikkoshi-ya de arubaito o shimasu. | Shouta works part-time at a moving company. |
| トラックにはこを運びます。 | Torakku ni hako o hakobimasu. | We carry boxes to the truck. |
| はことれいぞうこをトラックで運びます。 | Hako to reizōko o torakku de hakobimasu. | We'll transport the boxes and the fridge by truck. |

---

### Noun + の後 — "After [noun]"

Used to describe what happens **after** a given event or noun.

- **Structure:** `[Noun]` + の後、`[Result clause]`
- **Key Phrase:** あいさつの後、引っこしを始めます。*(Aisatsu no ato, hikkoshi o hajimemasu.)* — "After greeting each other, we will start moving."

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| 朝起きたらあいさつをします。 | Asa okitara aisatsu o shimasu. | I'll greet them when I wake up in the morning. |
| 近所の人にあいさつに行きます。 | Kinjo no hito ni aisatsu ni ikimasu. | I'll go and greet my neighbours. |

---

### 〜終わったら — "When [action] is finished"

Used to express that another action will take place **after completing** something.

- **Structure:** `[Verb te-form stem]` + 終わったら、`[next action]`
- **Key Phrase:** はこをぜんぶ運び終わったら、そうじします。*(Hako o zenbu hakobi owattara, sōji shimasu.)* — "I'll clean when I've finished moving all the boxes."

---

## 3. Learning Context

- **Topic:** Moving day — vocabulary, customs, and connecting actions with reasons and sequence
- **Key Structures:** Verb noun formation (dropping ます); から (reason); noun + の後 (after); 〜終わったら (after finishing)
- **Cultural Note:** **引っこしそば** — In Japan, people traditionally gave soba noodles to new neighbours on moving day. そば also means "close/near", and long noodles represent a long relationship. Today many people simply eat soba themselves on moving day.
