## 2. Grammar & Sentences

### （noun）は（noun）がいいです — Describing Advantages

Used to highlight a good quality or benefit of something.

- **Structure:** `[subject]` は `[noun]` がいいです
- **Example:** 今の仕事はきゅう料がいいです。*(Ima no shigoto wa kyūryō ga ii desu.)* — "My current job offers a good salary."

---

### （verb potential）といいです — Expressing Wishes

Used to express a desire or wish for something to happen.

- **Structure:** `[Verb dictionary/potential form]` + といいです
- **Example:** 本屋で働けるといいです。*(Hon'ya de hatarakeru to ii desu.)* — "It would be good if I could work in a bookshop."

---

### から vs ので — Explaining Reasons

Both から and ので mean "because", used to give a reason.

- **から (kara):** more direct / casual
- **ので (node):** softer / slightly more formal

| Structure | Example | Translation |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| …だからです | 理ゆうは、まんがやアニメが好きだからです。*(Riyū wa, manga ya anime ga suki dakara desu.)* | "The reason is because I like manga and anime." |
| …ので、… | 本を読むのが好きなので、おうぼしました。*(Hon wo yomu no ga suki nanode, ōbo shimashita.)* | "Because I like reading, I applied for this position." |

---

### Long Vowel Rule — う extending "o" sounds

When a hiragana syllable ends in "o", adding う lengthens the vowel sound.

- **Example:** おうぼ (応募) — the お sound in おう is lengthened → *ōbo*
- Other examples: きゅう料 (*kyūryō*), そうですか (*sō desu ka*)

---

### Job Hunting Sentences in Context

- 正社員の仕事をさがしています。*(Seishain no shigoto wo sagashite imasu.)* — "We are looking for a permanent job."
- この会社は正社員の求人があります。*(Kono kaisha wa seishain no kyūjin ga arimasu.)* — "This company has a permanent position available."
- 面せつのときに、りれき書を持ってきてください。*(Mensetsu no toki ni, rirekisho wo motte kite kudasai.)* — "Please bring your CV when you come to the interview."
- 週に四日働けます。*(Shū ni yokka hatarakemasu.)* — "I can work 4 days a week."

> **Culture Note:** In Japan, only a りれき書 (CV) is typically required — a cover letter is not standard practice.
