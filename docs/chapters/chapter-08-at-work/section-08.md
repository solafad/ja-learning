## 2. Housing Type Comparison

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| いっこだて／一戸建て | ikkodate | detached house (standalone building) |
| マンション | manshon | apartment in a multi-storey building |
| アパート | apāto | small apartment (usually 2-storey, wooden structure) |

> **Note:** マンション in Japanese does NOT mean "mansion" — it simply refers to a flat in a large multi-storey concrete building. アパート is smaller and often wooden.

---

## 3. Grammar & Sentences

### 〜に住んでいます — "I am living in…"

- **Structure:** `[place]` + に + 住んでいます
- **Note:** The correct spelling for "I am living" is 住んでいます (not 住いています).
- **Example:** 友だちと住んでいます。*(Tomodachi to sunde imasu.)* — "I am living with a friend."
- **Example:** 一こ建てに住んでいます。*(Ikkodate ni sunde imasu.)* — "I am living in a detached house."
- **Example:** このアパートは古いですが、駅にちかいです。*(Kono apāto wa furui desu ga, eki ni chikai desu.)* — "This small apartment is old but close to the station."

---

### 〜に引っこす — "To move to…"

- **Structure:** `[place]` + に + 引っこします
- **Example:** マンションに引（ひ）っこします。*(Manshon ni hikko shimasu.)* — "He will move to a multi-storey apartment."
- **Example:** 先月、このマンションに引っこしました。*(Sengetsu, kono manshon ni hikkoshimashita.)* — "She moved into this multi-storey apartment last month."

---

### 〜が建てられました — Passive: "was built"

- **Structure:** `[subject]` + が + 建てられました
- **Example:** あそこに新しいマンションが建てられました。*(Asoko ni atarashii manshon ga tateraremashita.)* — "A new multi-storey apartment was built over there."

---

### （noun）つきの — "A … with …"

Used to describe a property that includes a specific feature.

- **Structure:** `[feature noun]` + つきの + `[main noun]`
- **Example:** これは庭つきの一こ建てです。*(Kore wa niwa-tsuki no ikkodate desu.)* — "This is a detached house with a garden."
- **Note:** To add more details, use the structure （noun）つきの（house） to say "a house with…".

---

### 〜てはいけません — "You may not / must not"

Used to express prohibition.

- **Structure:** Verb て form + `はいけません`
- **Example:** アパートで猫といっしょに住んではいけません。*(Apāto de neko to issho ni sunde wa ikemasen.)* — "You may not live in the small apartment if you have cats."

---

### ぼく — Male first-person pronoun

- ぼく is used by male speakers as a casual way to say "I".
- **Example:** ここはぼくの部屋です。*(Koko wa boku no heya desu.)* — "This is my room."

## 5. Learning Context

- **Topic:** Japanese housing vocabulary — three types of homes and describing where you live
- **Culture Note:** In Japan, マンション does NOT mean "mansion" — it means a flat in a large reinforced concrete building. アパート is typically smaller, often 2-storey and wooden. いっこだて is a standalone detached house.
- **Grammar Focus:** 〜に住んでいます (living in); 〜に引っこす (to move to); 〜が建てられました (passive: was built); （noun）つきの (with a feature); 〜てはいけません (prohibition)
