## 2. Grammar & Sentences

### という — "called / named"

Used when introducing a name that the listener may **not be familiar** with.

- **Structure:** `[Name]` + という + `[Noun]`
- **Example:** さくらという銀（ぎん）行 — "a bank called Sakura"
- **Example:** さくらという銀（ぎん）行に行きます。*(Sakura to iu ginkō ni ikimasu.)* — "I will go to a bank called Sakura."
- **Example:** パン屋はローソンというコンビニのとなりです。*(Pan'ya wa Rōson to iu konbini no tonari desu.)* — "The bakery is next to a convenience store called Lawson."
- **Example:** この本は、なつめそうせきという人が書いた。*(Kono hon wa, Natsume Sōseki to iu hito ga kaita.)* — "This book was written by someone called Natsume Soseki."

---

### っていう — Casual form of という

In casual speech, という contracts to っていう.

| Register | Form | Example |
|----------|------|---------|
| Formal | という | ポチという犬 |
| Casual | っていう | ポチっていう犬 |

- **Example:** うちにはポチっていう犬がいる。*(Uchi ni wa Pochi tte iu inu ga iru.)* — "We have a dog called Pochi in our house."
- **Example:** ワンピースっていうまんがが好き。*(Wanpīsu tte iu manga ga suki.)* — "I like a manga called One Piece."
- **Example:** Busuuっていうアプリで日本語を勉強してるよ。*(Busuu tte iu apuri de nihongo wo benkyō shiteru yo.)* — "I am studying Japanese with an app called Busuu."
- **Example:** まつりっていうレストランで日本料理を食べた。*(Matsuri tte iu resutoran de nihon ryōri wo tabeta.)* — "We ate Japanese food in a restaurant called Matsuri."
- **Example:** この本は、なつめそうせきっていう人が書いた。*(Kono hon wa, Natsume Sōseki tte iu hito ga kaita.)* — "This book was written by someone called Natsume Soseki."

---

### When to use という vs さくら銀行

| Form | Usage | Example |
|------|-------|---------|
| さくらという銀行 | Listener is **not familiar** with the name | Introducing Sakura Bank for the first time |
| さくら銀行 | Listener **is familiar** with the name | Referring to a bank already known to both speakers |

> **Real example:** The female speaker used っていう when talking about なっとう because she assumed the male speaker was **unfamiliar** with it and needed the label explained.

---

### という with fees and labels

という can also be used to label or define something:

- 週末は手すう料というお金をはらいます。*(Shūmatsu wa tesūryō to iu okane wo haraimasu.)* — "We pay a fee called commission on weekends."

---

## 3. Culture Note: Bank Hours in Japan

In Japan, banks are usually open from **9am to 3pm, Monday to Friday**.

- ATMs can be used at **any time**.
- However, using an ATM **outside opening hours** results in a **higher 手数（すう）料 (commission fee)**.
- Tip: Use ATMs during business hours to avoid extra charges.
