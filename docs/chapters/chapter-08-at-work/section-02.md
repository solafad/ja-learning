## 2. Grammar & Sentences

**～ので — Giving a reason ("because / so"):** Attaches to a plain verb or adjective to explain the cause of an action.

- **Structure:** `[Verb plain form]` + ので、`[result]`
- **Example:** あの会社のきゅう人を見たので、おうぼします。*(Ano kaisha no kyūjin wo mita node, ōbo shimasu.)* — "I saw that company's job advertisement, so I will apply."

---

**～について — "About / regarding":** Indicates the topic being discussed or asked about.

- **Structure:** `[Noun]` + について + verb
- **Example:** ここで働きたい理ゆうについて教えてください。*(Koko de hatarakitai riyū ni tsuite oshiete kudasai.)* — "Please tell me the reason why you want to work here."
- **Example:** めんせつで、きゅう料とおうぼの理ゆうについてしつもんします。*(Mensetsu de, kyūryō to ōbo no riyū ni tsuite shitsumon shimasu.)* — "At an interview, I ask questions about the salary and their reason for applying."

---

**しつれいします — Polite interview etiquette:** This phrase is used when entering or leaving a room in a formal/professional context.

- **Entering a room:** しつれいします。*(Shitsurei shimasu.)* — "Excuse me. I will come in."
- **Sitting down:** しつれいします。*(Shitsurei shimasu.)* — "Excuse me. I will sit down."
- **Note:** Incorrect alternatives in this context are 何も言いません (say nothing) and さようなら (goodbye).

---

**Interview Sentences in Context:**

- りれき書は持っていますか。*(Rirekisho wa motte imasu ka.)* — "Do you have a CV?"
- めんせつのじゅんびをします。*(Mensetsu no junbi wo shimasu.)* — "I will prepare for the interview."
- 明日めんせつがあります。*(Ashita mensetsu ga arimasu.)* — "I have an interview tomorrow."
- おうぼの理ゆうは何ですか。*(Ōbo no riyū wa nan desu ka.)* — "What is your reason for applying?"
- 理ゆうを教えてください。*(Riyū wo oshiete kudasai.)* — "Please tell me the reason."
- きゅう料はいくらですか。*(Kyūryō wa ikura desu ka.)* — "How much is the salary?"
- きゅう料は一時間千二百円です。*(Kyūryō wa ichijikan sen nihyaku en desu.)* — "The salary is 1,200 yen per hour."
- ラーメン屋でアルバイトをします。*(Rāmen'ya de arubaito wo shimasu.)* — "I work part-time at a ramen bar."

## 3. Learning Context

- **Topic:** Job interview vocabulary and formal etiquette in Japanese workplaces
- **Culture Note:** しつれいします is an essential phrase for professional settings — it signals respectful awareness before entering a room, sitting down, or leaving. Omitting it in a formal interview context would be considered impolite.
- **Grammar Focus:** ～ので for stating reasons; ～について for indicating a topic; て form + います for current states
