## 2. Grammar & Sentences

### 〜を開く — "To open (an account)"

開く (*hiraku*) — to open — is used for opening shops, flowers, and bank accounts.

- **Example:** 口座を開きます。*(Kōza wo hirakimasu.)* — "She will open a bank account."
- **Example:** 花屋は朝九時に開きます。*(Hanaya wa asa kuji ni hirakimasu.)* — "The flower shop will be open at 9am."
- **Note:** 花屋 = what a shop sells (花) + 屋 → "a flower shop"

---

### 〜たいんですが — Politely stating a wish

A softened, polite form of 〜たいです used when making requests at service counters.

- **Structure:** `[Verb stem]` + たいんですが
- **Example:** すみません、口座を開きたいんですが。*(Sumimasen, kōza wo hirakitain desu ga.)* — "Excuse me, I would like to open a bank account."

---

### 〜を送る — "To send"

送る (*okuru*) — to send — used for money, emails, and parcels.

- **Example:** 友だちにメールを送ります。*(Tomodachi ni meeru wo okurimasu.)* — "I will send an email to a friend."
- **Example:** 月に一回家族にお金をおくります。*(Tsuki ni ikkai kazoku ni okane wo okurimasu.)* — "I will send money to my family once a month."

---

### 〜とき — "When / at the time of"

Used to connect two actions, meaning "when (X happens), (Y)".

- **Structure:** `[Verb dictionary form]` + とき、`[result]`
- **Example:** ぎん行に行くとき、はんこを持っていきます。*(Ginkō ni iku toki, hanko wo motte ikimasu.)* — "I will take my seal when I go to the bank."
- **Example:** 口座を開くとき、何をじゅんびしますか。*(Kōza wo hiraku toki, nani wo junbi shimasu ka.)* — "What do I need to prepare to open a bank account?"
- **Example:** お金を送るとき、手すう料をはらいます。*(Okane wo okuru toki, tesūryō wo haraimasu.)* — "When you send money, you will pay a commission."

---

### 〜があれば — "If I have / If there is"

Conditional ば form of ある (*aru*), meaning "if (X) exists/is available".

- **Example:** 口座があれば、家族にお金をおくれます。*(Kōza ga areba, kazoku ni okane wo okuremasu.)* — "If I have a bank account, I will be able to send money to my family."

---

## 3. Culture Note: はんこ (Personal Seal)

**はんこ（判子）** or **いんかん（印鑑）** are Japanese personal seals used **instead of signatures** on official documents.

- A person's surname is engraved on the はんこ.
- Used to stamp important documents such as bank or house contracts.
- You can have your own はんこ made at a はんこ shop.
- When opening a bank account, you typically bring your **はんこ and パスポート**.

| Situation | What to bring |
|-----------|--------------|
| Opening a bank account | はんことパスポートを持って行きます — "You will bring your seal and passport." |
| Stamping documents | ここにはんこをおしてください — "Please stamp your personal seal here." |

## 4. Sentence Examples

- 明日銀行に行きます。*(Ashita ginkō ni ikimasu.)* — "I will go to the bank tomorrow."
- 新しいゲームに五千円はらいました。*(Atarashii gēmu ni gosen en haraimashita.)* — "She paid 5,000 yen for a new game."
- 手すう料は五百三十円です。*(Tesūryō wa gohyaku sanjū en desu.)* — "The commission is 530 yen."
- 手すう料をはらいます。*(Tesūryō wo haraimasu.)* — "She will pay a commission."
- はんことパスポートを持って行きます。*(Hanko to pasupōto wo motte ikimasu.)* — "You will bring your seal and passport."

## 5. Learning Context

- **Topic:** Banking vocabulary and everyday money transactions in Japan

- **Grammar Focus:** 〜たいんですが for polite requests; 〜とき for "when"; 〜があれば for "if I have"; 送る for sending money/messages
