## 2. Grammar & Sentences

### Appliance-specific verbs

Different verbs are used depending on which appliance you are operating:

| Action | Verb | Example | Translation |
|--------|------|---------|-------------|
| Use the washing machine | 洗たく機を**使う** | 洗たく機を使います。 | I put the washing machine on. |
| Use the microwave | 電子レンジで**あたためる** | 電子レンジでおべんとうをあたためます。 | I warm up bento in the microwave. |
| Use the vacuum cleaner | そうじ機を**かける** | そうじ機をかけます。 | I use a vacuum cleaner. |

> **Note:** **かける** specifically means "to run / to use" when referring to a vacuum cleaner. **使います** is used for general use of the washing machine. **あたためる** specifically means to heat or warm food.

---

### もう + past tense — "Already"

Used to state that something **has already been done**.

- **Structure:** もう + `[Verb past tense]`
- **Key Phrase:** 洗たく機はもう買いました。*(Sentakuki wa mō kaimashita.)* — "We've already bought a washing machine."

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| 洗たく機はもう買いました。新しいそうじ機と電子レンジがほしいです。 | Sentakuki wa mō kaimashita. Atarashii sōjiki to denshi renji ga hoshii desu. | We've already bought a washing machine. We want a new vacuum cleaner and a microwave. |

---

### たら — "If / When" (conditional)

Used to express a condition: *"if you do X, then Y will happen."* Review from Chapter 6.

- **Structure:** `[Verb ta-form stem]` + たら + `[Result]`
- **Key Phrase:** 電子レンジであたためたらおいしいですよ。*(Denshi renji de atatametara oishii desu yo.)* — "If you warm this in the microwave, it will be delicious."

### Example Sentences

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| ちかさんは大きいれいぞうこを買いました。 | Chika-san wa ōkii reizōko o kaimashita. | Chika bought a big fridge. |

| 週まつは、そうじ機をかけて洗たく機を使います。 | Shūmatsu wa, sōjiki o kakete sentakuki o tsukaimasu. | At the weekend, I vacuum and put the washing machine on. |

## 3. Learning Context

- **Topic:** Naming and using electrical appliances; describing daily household chores
- **Key Structures:** Appliance-specific verbs (使う, あたためる, かける); もう + past tense (already); たら conditional
- **Common mistake:** Using **使います** for the vacuum cleaner ✗ — the correct verb is **そうじ機をかける** ✓
- **Cultural Note:** **日本のトイレ** — Japanese toilets are famous for their many features: heated seats, a spray to wash yourself, and even a built-in dryer, so you don't need to worry about running out of toilet paper!
