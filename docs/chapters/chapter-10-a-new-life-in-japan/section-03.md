## 2. Grammar & Sentences

### Transitive vs Intransitive verbs

Japanese has **pairs of verbs** for many actions — one that describes a **person doing something** (transitive), and one that describes a **thing happening by itself** (intransitive). The particle used is the key signal.

| Type | Verb | Particle | Meaning | Example |
|------|------|----------|---------|---------|
| Transitive (他動詞) | 開ける・閉める | **を** | someone opens/closes something | はつみさんはドアを**開けます**。 |
| Intransitive (自動詞) | 開く・閉まる | **が** | the thing opens/closes by itself | ドア**が開きます**。 |

> **Rule summary:**
> - **を + 開ける / 閉める** → someone performs the action on an object
> - **が + 開く / 閉まる** → the thing does the action by itself (automatic, natural)

### 開ける vs 開く — "To open"

| Verb | Reading | Type | Particle | Use case |
|------|---------|------|----------|----------|
| 開ける | あける | Transitive | を | A person opens something |
| 開く | あく | Intransitive | が | Something opens by itself |

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| まどを開けますね。わあ、ふじ山！ | Mado o akemasu ne. Wā, Fujisan! | I'll open the window. Wow, Mt Fuji! |
| はつみさんはドアを開けます。 | Hatsumi-san wa doa o akemasu. | Hatsumi will open the door. |
| はつみさんはれいぞうこを開けます。 | Hatsumi-san wa reizōko o akemasu. | Hatsumi will open the fridge. |
| ドアが開きます。 | Doa ga akimasu. | The door will open (by itself). |
| 電車が止まって、ドアが開きます。 | Densha ga tomatte, doa ga akimasu. | The train stops and the door opens (automatically). |
| ドアが開いたら、電車に乗ります。 | Doa ga aitara, densha ni norimasu. | If the door opens, we'll get on the train. |
| プレゼントを開けてもいいですか。 | Purezento o akete mo ii desu ka. | May I open the present? |

### 閉める vs 閉まる — "To close"

| Verb | Reading | Type | Particle | Use case |
|------|---------|------|----------|----------|
| 閉める | しめる | Transitive | を | A person closes something |
| 閉まる | しまる | Intransitive | が | Something closes by itself |

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| ドアを閉めます。 | Doa o shimemasu. | I will close the door. |
| さとしさんは、仕事に行くときドアを閉めます。 | Satoshi-san wa, shigoto ni iku toki doa o shimemasu. | Satoshi will close the door when he goes to work. |
| さむいから、まどを閉めてください。 | Samui kara, mado o shimete kudasai. | Because it's cold, please close the window. |
| はつみさんはまどを閉めます。 | Hatsumi-san wa mado o shimemasu. | Hatsumi will close the window. |
| れいぞうこを閉めてください。 | Reizōko o shimete kudasai. | Please close the fridge. |
| ドアが閉まります。 | Doa ga shimarimasu. | The door will close (by itself). |
| マンションのドアが閉まります。 | Manshon no doa ga shimarimasu. | The apartment door will close (by itself). |
| ドアが閉まります。ちゅう意してください。 | Doa ga shimarimasu. Chūi shite kudasai. | The door will close. Please be careful. |
| まどが閉まります。 | Mado ga shimarimasu. | The window will close (by itself). |
| ゲートが十分後に閉まります。急いでください。 | Gēto ga juppun go ni shimarimasu. Isoide kudasai. | The gate will close in 10 minutes. Please hurry. |

### Check the particle!

| Sentence | Particle | Why? |
|----------|----------|------|
| まどを**開けます** | を | Someone (person) opens the window |
| まどが**開きます** | が | The window opens by itself |
| まどを**閉めます** | を | Someone (person) closes the window |
| まどが**閉まります** | が | The window closes by itself |

## 3. Learning Context

- **Topic:** Transitive and intransitive verb pairs — the difference between doing something to an object vs. something happening by itself
- **Key Distinction:** を + transitive (person acts on object) vs が + intransitive (thing acts on its own)
- **New vocabulary:** あく / 開く (intransitive "to open"), しまる / 閉まる (intransitive "to close")
- **Common mistake:** Using を with 開く or 閉まる ✗ — intransitive verbs use が, not を ✓
