## 2. Grammar & Sentences

### Four-verb set: on / off — transitive and intransitive

This lesson extends the transitive/intransitive pair pattern from Section 3 to cover **turning things on and off**.

| Action | Verb | Type | Particle | Meaning |
|--------|------|------|----------|---------|
| turn on | つける | Transitive | を | someone turns something on |
| come on | つく | Intransitive | が | something comes on by itself |
| turn off | 消す | Transitive | を | someone turns something off |
| go out | 消える | Intransitive | が | something goes out by itself |

---

### つける vs つく — "To turn on"

- **Structure (transitive):** `(someone)` は + `(something)` を + つけます
- **Structure (intransitive):** `(something)` が + つきます

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| けいたさんは電気をつけます。 | Keita-san wa denki o tsukemasu. | Keita will turn on the light. |
| 部屋の電気をつけます。 | Heya no denki o tsukemasu. | I'll turn on the light in the room. |
| テレビをつけます。 | Terebi o tsukemasu. | I'll turn on the TV. |
| 電気がつきます。 | Denki ga tsukimasu. | The light will come on (by itself). |
| テレビがつきます。 | Terebi ga tsukimasu. | The TV will come on (by itself). |
| 映画が終わったら、電気がつきます。 | Eiga ga owattara, denki ga tsukimasu. | When the film ends, the lights will come on. |
| コンピューターがつきますよ。 | Konpyūtā ga tsukimasu yo. | The computer will come on. |

> **Note:** Even though *we* press the power button on a computer, the **switch** makes the computer come on by itself — so we use **つきます** (intransitive) for the computer turning on.

---

### 消す vs 消える — "To turn off / to go out"

- **Structure (transitive):** `(someone)` は + `(something)` を + 消します
- **Structure (intransitive):** `(something)` が + 消えます

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| けいたさんは電気を消します。 | Keita-san wa denki o keshimasu. | Keita will turn off the light. |
| ちかさんは、テレビを消します。 | Chika-san wa, terebi o keshimasu. | Chika will turn off the TV. |
| 帰るときは、コンピューターを消してください。 | Kaeru toki wa, konpyūtā o keshite kudasai. | Please turn off the computer when you go back home. |
| 会社に行くとき、まどを閉めて電気を消します。 | Kaisha ni iku toki, mado o shimete denki o keshimasu. | When I go to work, I close the window and turn off the lights. |
| 電気が消えます。 | Denki ga kiemasu. | The light will go out (by itself). |
| 電気が消えて、暗くなりました。 | Denki ga kiete, kurai ni narimashita. | The light went out and it got dark. |
| 消えました。 | Kiemashita. | (The light) has gone out. |
| だれもいないとき電気が消えます。 | Dare mo inai toki denki ga kiemasu. | When nobody is there, the light will go out. |

---

### だれも + negative — "Nobody"

**だれも** followed by a **negative verb** means "nobody".

- **Structure:** だれも + `[negative verb]`
- **Key Phrase:** だれもいないとき電気が消えます。*(Dare mo inai toki denki ga kiemasu.)* — "When nobody is there, the light will go out."

> **Note:** だれも by itself means "anyone/everyone". The negative verb turns it into "nobody".

---

### てもいいです — "You may / It's okay to…" (Permission)

Review: used to give permission for someone to do something.

- **Structure:** `[Verb て-form]` + もいいです
- **Key Phrase:** まどを開けてもいいですよ。*(Mado o akete mo ii desu yo.)* — "You may also open the window."

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| あつかったら、エアコンをつけてください。まどを開けてもいいですよ。 | Atsukattara, eakon o tsukete kudasai. Mado o akete mo ii desu yo. | Please turn on the air-conditioner if it is hot. You may also open the window. |
| サッカーを見たいから、テレビをつけてください。 | Sakkā o mitai kara, terebi o tsukete kudasai. | Because I want to watch football, please turn on the TV. |

---

### Particle check summary

| Sentence | Particle | Why? |
|----------|----------|------|
| 電気**を**つけます | を | Someone turns the light on |
| 電気**が**つきます | が | The light comes on by itself |
| 電気**を**消します | を | Someone turns the light off |
| 電気**が**消えます | が | The light goes out by itself |

## 3. Learning Context

- **Topic:** Extending transitive/intransitive pairs to "turn on" (つける/つく) and "turn off" (消す/消える)
- **Key Structures:** を + transitive (someone acts); が + intransitive (thing acts by itself); だれも + negative = "nobody"; てもいいです for permission
- **Common mistake:** Using を with 消えます ✗ (e.g. 電気を消えます) — 消える is intransitive, must use が ✓
- **Tricky case:** Computer turning on uses **つきます** (intransitive) because the machine activates by itself once you press the switch
