## 2. Grammar & Sentences

### 屋（や）— "Shop / Company" suffix

Adding **屋（や）** after a product or service creates the name of the shop or company that provides it.

| Base word | Meaning | + 屋 | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|------|---------|
| クリーニング | dry cleaning | クリーニング屋 | dry cleaner's |
| 不動さん | property | 不動さん屋 | estate agency |
| はんこ | personal seal | はんこ屋 | personal seal shop |
| 本 | book | 本屋 | bookshop |
| 文ぼうぐ | stationery | 文ぼうぐ屋 | stationery shop |
| 引っこし | moving (house) | 引っこし屋 | moving company |

> **Review:** We learned **屋** with moving companies in Chapter 9. The same pattern applies to all shops and services.

---

### 屋さん — Adding さん for respect or affection

Adding **さん** after **屋** shows **respect or affection** for the shop. This is common in casual speech.

- **Structure:** `[Service]` + 屋さん
- **Key Phrase:** 今日の朝、クリーニング屋さんに行ったよ。*(Kyō no asa, kurīningu-ya-san ni itta yo.)* — "I went to the dry cleaner's this morning."

---

### 屋 for personality types

**屋** is also used to describe **personal characteristics** — the kind of person someone is.

| Japanese | Romaji | Meaning |
|----------|--------|---------|
| がんばり屋 | ganbari-ya | a hard worker |
| さびしがり屋 | sabishigari-ya | someone who doesn't like being alone |

- **Example:** 田中さんはがんばり屋です。*(Tanaka-san wa ganbari-ya desu.)* — "Ms Tanaka is a hard worker."

## 3. Learning Context

- **Topic:** Local shops and services in Japan; the 屋 suffix for shop names and personality types
- **Key Structures:** `[Service/product]` + 屋 (shop name); 屋さん (respectful/affectionate form); 屋 for personality descriptions
- **Key Distinction:** **クリーニング屋** (the shop) vs **クリーニング屋さん** (same shop, but warmer/more casual tone)
- **Cultural Note:** **はんこ（判子）** — A personal stamp used instead of a handwritten signature in Japan. Needed for official documents such as bank account opening, apartment rental contracts, and job applications. Getting one made at a **はんこ屋** is one of the first steps when settling into life in Japan.
