## 2. Grammar & Sentences

**Conditional "if" with と:** Used to express natural or automatic consequences — "if X happens, then Y follows."

- **Structure:** `casual form (present tense)` + `と、` + result
- **Key Phrase:** 天気がいいと、ふじ山が見えます。*(Tenki ga ii to, Fujisan ga miemasu.)* — "If the weather is good, you can see Mt. Fuji."

**Examples from the lesson:**

- スイッチを押すと、電気がつきます。*(Suicchi wo osu to, denki ga tsukimasu.)* — "If you press the switch, the light will come on."
- スイッチを押すと、電気が消えます。*(Suicchi wo osu to, denki ga kiemasu.)* — "If you press the switch, the light goes out."
- このボタンを押すと、ドアが閉まります。*(Kono botan wo osu to, doa ga shimarimasu.)* — "If you press this button, the door will close."
- ここを押すと、上に行けます。*(Koko wo osu to, ue ni ikemasu.)* — "If you press here, you can go up."
- ここを押すと、電気がつきます。*(Koko wo osu to, denki ga tsukimasu.)* — "If you press here, the light will come on."
- このスイッチを押すと、映画が見られます。*(Kono suicchi wo osu to, eiga ga miraremasu.)* — "If you press this switch, you can watch a film."
- このボタンを押すと、音が大きくなったり、小さくなったりします。*(Kono botan wo osu to, oto ga ookiku nattari, chiisaku nattari shimasu.)* — "If you press this button, the volume increases and decreases."
- これを使うと、すずしくなります。*(Kore wo tsukau to, suzushiku narimasu.)* — "If you use this, it'll get cooler."
- トイレのドアを開けると、電気がつきます。*(Toire no doa wo akeru to, denki ga tsukimasu.)* — "If you open the toilet door, the light will come on."
- モニターを見ると、着く時間が分かります。*(Monitaa wo miru to, tsuku jikan ga wakarimasu.)* — "If you look at the monitor, you'll see the time of arrival."
- 飛行機がおくれると、電車にのれません。*(Hikouki ga okureru to, densha ni noremasen.)* — "If the flight is delayed, I can't get on the train."
- 大さかへ行く便は三時間おくれています。*(Oosaka e iku bin wa sanjikan okurete imasu.)* — "The flight to Osaka is delayed for 3 hours."

## 3. Learning Context

- **Grammar Focus:** The と conditional expresses automatic or natural results — when A happens, B inevitably follows.
- **Usage Note:** Unlike たら or ば, と is commonly used for mechanical/natural consequences (e.g. pressing buttons, weather effects).
- **Context:** Lesson set on a plane — exploring seat controls, monitors, and flight scenarios.
- **Cultural Note:** Mt. Fuji (ふじ山) can be seen from flights on certain Tokyo routes. Japanese airlines often make in-flight announcements about it, and some provide seat guides for the best aerial views.
