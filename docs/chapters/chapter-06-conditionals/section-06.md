## 2. Grammar & Sentences

### Transport Verb Trio

| Verb | Reading | Meaning |
|------|---------|---------|
| 乗る | のる | to get on / ride |
| 降りる | おりる | to get off |
| 乗り換える | のりかえる | to change / transfer |

**Particle rule:**
- Use **に** with 乗（の）る → バス**に**乗ります。*(I get on a bus.)*
- Use **を** with 降（お）りる → バス**を**降ります。*(I get off the bus.)*

**Examples:**
- 上野駅で電車を乗り換えます。*(Ueno-eki de densha wo norikaemasu.)* — "I change trains at Ueno Station."
- 新ばし駅で電車をのりかえます。*(Shinbashi-eki de densha wo norikaemasu.)* — "I change trains at Shinbashi Station."
- 新宿駅で電車を降ります。*(Shinjuku-eki de densha wo orimasu.)* — "I get off the train at Shinjuku Station."
- 公園の前でバスに乗って、ゆう便きょくでバスを降ります。*(Kouen no mae de basu ni notte, yuubinkyoku de basu wo orimasu.)* — "I get on a bus in front of the park, and I get off the bus at the post office."

---

### 間（ま）に合う — "To be in time for"

- バスに間に合いますか。*(Basu ni maniau masu ka?)* — "Will I be in time for the bus?"
- 六時の新かんせんに間に合いますか。*(Rokuji no shinkansen ni maniau masu ka?)* — "Will I be in time for the 6 o'clock shinkansen?"
- 新かんせんに間に合いません。いそいでください。*(Shinkansen ni maniamasen. Isoide kudasai.)* — "We won't be in time for the shinkansen. Please hurry."
- 時間がないので、急ぎます。*(Jikan ga nai no de, isogimasu.)* — "Because I don't have time, I'll hurry."

---

### The ば Form — Conjugation of いそぐ

| Form | Japanese | Romaji | Notes |
|------|----------|--------|-------|
| て form | いそいで | isoide | used with ください for requests |
| ば form | いそげば | isogeba | conditional "if you hurry" |
| Negative | いそがない | isoganai | plain negative |

**Formation rule for う-verbs ending in ぐ:** change ぐ → げば  
いそ**ぐ** → いそ**げば**

---

### 〜ば、よかったです — Expressing Regrets

**Meaning:** "I wish I had…" / "It would have been good if…"  
**Structure:** `ば form` + `よかったです`

This pattern expresses **regret** about something you *should have done* but didn't.

- 急（いそ）げば、よかったです。*(Isogeba, yokatta desu.)* — "I wish I had hurried." / "He wished that he had hurried."

---

### までに — "By" (Deadline/Time Limit)

**Meaning:** by [time] — indicates a deadline  
**Structure:** `time expression` + `までに`

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| 十二時までに、地下てつにのりたいです。 | Juuniji made ni, chikatetsu ni noritai desu. | I want to get on the metro by 12. |
| 来週の木曜日までに、旅行のじゅんびをします。 | Raishuu no mokuyoubi made ni, ryokou no junbi wo shimasu. | I'll prepare for the trip by Thursday next week. |
| 夜の九時までに、ホテルに着きたいです。 | Yoru no kuji made ni, hoteru ni tsukitai desu. | I want to arrive at the hotel by 9 at night. |

**Note:** までに (by a point in time) vs まで (until/up to a point in time) — まで focuses on duration, までに sets a deadline.

## 3. Learning Context

- **Grammar Focus:** The ば conditional form introduced via transport scenarios — 急げば (if you hurry) leading into the regret pattern 〜ば、よかったです.
- **Key Pattern:** 〜ば、よかったです = "I wish I had…" (past regret).
- **Particle Rule:** 乗る takes **に**, 降りる takes **を** — opposite of English intuition.
- **New Particle:** までに = "by" for deadlines, distinct from まで = "until."
- **Context:** Train stations in Tokyo (Ueno, Shinbashi, Shinjuku), shinkansen timetables, missing transport connections.
- **Cultural Note:** Japanese trains are famously punctual — missing a scheduled shinkansen by even seconds is a real concern, making 間に合う and 急ぐ essential vocabulary for daily life.
