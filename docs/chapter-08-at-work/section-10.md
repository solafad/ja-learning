## 2. Grammar & Sentences

### だけ — "Only" (neutral / satisfied)

Place **だけ** directly after a noun or number. No change to the verb form.

- **Structure:** `[Noun / number]` + だけ + `[verb / です]`
- **Use when:** the speaker is **neutral or satisfied** with the quantity/situation

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| 部屋が一つだけあります。 | Heya ga hitotsu dake arimasu. | There is only one room. |
| 休みは日曜日だけです。 | Yasumi wa nichiyōbi dake desu. | My only day off is Sunday. |
| 週に１日だけアルバイトをします。 | Shū ni ichinichi dake arubaito wo shimasu. | She only works one day a week. |
| 朝ごはんは食べません。コーヒーだけ飲みます。 | Asagohan wa tabemasen. Kōhī dake nomimasu. | I don't eat breakfast. I only drink coffee. |
| 朝六時に起きるので、一時間だけゲームをします。 | Asa rokuji ni okiru node, ichijikan dake gēmu wo shimasu. | Because I wake up at 6am, I play video games for only an hour. |
| 私だけです。 | Watashi dake desu. | It's only me. |

> **Note:** 朝六時に起きるので → she decided to play for only 1 hour and is **happy** with that, so we use **だけ**.

---

### しか — "Only" (implies not enough / dissatisfied)

Place **しか** after a noun or number. The verb **must be in the negative form**.

- **Structure:** `[Noun / number]` + しか + `[negative verb]`
- **Use when:** the speaker implies the amount is **too little / not enough**

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| 五百円しかありません。 | Gohyaku en shika arimasen. | I only have 500 yen (and it's not enough). |
| 休みは日曜日しかありません。 | Yasumi wa nichiyōbi shika arimasen. | The only day off is Sunday (and it's not enough). |
| ひらがなしか書けません。 | Hiragana shika kakemasen. | I can only write hiragana (and it's not enough). |
| 土曜日しか彼に会えません。 | Doyōbi shika kare ni aeemasen. | I can only see my boyfriend on Saturday (and I'm not happy about it). |
| 十分しかありません。 | Juppun shika arimasen. | I only have 10 minutes (and it's not enough). |
| このこたえはこんどうくんしか分かりません。 | Kono kotae wa Kondō-kun shika wakarimasen. | Only Kondō knows this answer. |

> **Key rule:** しか always requires a **negative** verb (ありません, できません, 分かりません, etc.)

---

### だけ vs しか — Side-by-Side Comparison

| Sentence | Meaning | Nuance |
|----------|---------|--------|
| 休みは日曜日**だけ**です。 | My only day off is Sunday. | Neutral / acceptable |
| 休みは日曜日**しか**ありません。 | The only day off is Sunday. | Not enough — unhappy |
| 一時間**だけ**ゲームをします。 | I play games for only an hour. | She decided this herself — content |
| 一時間**しか**ゲームをしません。 | I can only play games for an hour. | Wants to play longer — not enough |

---

### だけじゃなくて～も — "Not only X but also Y"

Used to say something includes more than just one thing.

- **Structure:** `[Noun]` + だけじゃなくて + `[Noun]` + も
- **Example:** 日曜日だけじゃなくて土曜日も休みです。*(Nichiyōbi dake ja nakute doyōbi mo yasumi desu.)* — "My days off are not only Sunday but also Saturday."
- **Example:** このちかくには、スーパーだけじゃなくてショッピングセンターもあります。*(Kono chikaku ni wa, sūpā dake ja nakute shoppingu sentā mo arimasu.)* — "There is not only a supermarket but also a shopping centre near here."
- **Example:** アパートだけじゃなくてマンションも見に行きます。*(Apāto dake ja nakute manshon mo mi ni ikimasu.)* — "We will visit not only small apartments but also multi-storey apartments."
- **Example:** ベッドだけじゃなくてテーブルもあります。*(Beddo dake ja nakute tēburu mo arimasu.)* — "There is not only a bed but also a table."
- **Example:** 私のアパートは古いけど、シャワーだけじゃなくておふろもある。*(Watashi no apāto wa furui kedo, shawā dake ja nakute ofuro mo aru.)* — "Although my apartment is old, there is not only a shower but also a bath."

---

## 3. Decision Guide: だけ or しか?

```
Is the speaker dissatisfied or implying "not enough"?
  YES → use しか + negative verb
  NO  → use だけ + any verb form
```

| Clue | Use |
|------|-----|
| Speaker chose the amount themselves and is fine with it | だけ |
| Speaker would prefer more / implies insufficient | しか |
| Verb is negative | Likely しか |
| Verb is positive | Likely だけ |

---

## 4. Learning Context

- **Topic:** Two words for "only" — だけ (neutral) vs しか (not enough); "not only X but also Y"
- **Grammar Focus:**
  - だけ + positive verb = neutral "only"
  - しか + **negative** verb = "only (and not enough)"
  - だけじゃなくて～も = "not only X but also Y"
