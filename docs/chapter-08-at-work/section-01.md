## 2. Grammar & Sentences

**て Form ＋ います — Ongoing Actions & Current States:** Used to describe something currently happening or a continuous situation.

- **Structure:** Verb て form + `います`
- **Key Phrase / Example:** 東京で仕事をさがしています。*(Tōkyō de shigoto wo sagashite imasu.)* — "I am looking for a job in Tokyo."

---

**パート vs アルバイト — Part-Time Work Nuances:** Both mean "part-time job" but differ in contract type.

- **パート** refers to a long-term contract with regular working hours — suits someone with other life commitments who wants a stable part-time role.
- **アルバイト** implies a brief contract with shorter, irregular hours — suits students.

| Term | Romaji | Meaning |
|------|--------|---------|
| 正社員（せいしゃいん） | seishain | permanent employee |
| パート | pāto | part-time (stable, long-term) |
| アルバイト | arubaito | part-time (flexible, for students) |

**Example Sentences:**
- やまとさんは正社員（せいしゃいん）です。*(Yamato-san wa seishain desu.)* — "Yamato is a permanent employee."
- スーパーでアルバイトをしています。*(Sūpā de arubaito wo shite imasu.)* — "I am working part-time in a supermarket."
- パン屋で働いています。*(Pan'ya de hataraite imasu.)* — "I am working in a bakery."

---

**Job Application Process — Key Vocabulary in Context:**

- いいきゅう人を見つけました。*(Ii kyūjin wo mitsukemashita.)* — "I found a good job advertisement."
- このきゅう人はどう思いますか。*(Kono kyūjin wa dō omoimasu ka.)* — "What do you think about this job advertisement?"
- りれき書を書きます。*(Rirekisho wo kakimasu.)* — "I will write my CV."
- りれき書の書き方を教えてください。*(Rirekisho no kakikata wo oshiete kudasai.)* — "Please teach me how to write a CV."
- この仕事におうぼします。*(Kono shigoto ni ōbo shimasu.)* — "I will apply for this job."
- 仕事におうぼする前に、りれき書を書きます。*(Shigoto ni ōbo suru mae ni, rirekisho wo kakimasu.)* — "Before applying for a job, I write my CV."

---

## 3. Learning Context

- **Topic:** Job hunting vocabulary and employment types in Japan
- **Culture Note:** きゅうじん (求人) are job advertisements often posted on signs, online boards, or dedicated job sites; bringing your りれきしょ (履歴書) when responding to a listing is standard practice.
- **Grammar Focus:** て form + います for ongoing actions; 前に (mae ni) for "before doing X"
