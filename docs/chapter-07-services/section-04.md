## 2. Grammar & Sentences

### Three-Way Review: てあげる / てくれる / てもらう

| Form | Match | Meaning |
|------|-------|---------|
| てあげる | giving a favour *(speaker is the **giver**)* | I do something for others |
| てくれる | giving a favour *(speaker is the **receiver**)* | someone does something for me |
| てもらう | receiving a favour | I get someone to do something for me |

**Same event, three perspectives — 見せる (to show) as a model:**

| Japanese | English | Form |
|----------|---------|------|
| 私は友だちに写真を見せて**あげます**。 | I show a photo to my friend. | あげる — I give the favour |
| 友だちは私に写真を見せて**くれます**。 | My friend shows me a photo. | くれる — friend gives to me |
| 私は友だちに写真を見せて**もらいます**。 | I get my friend to show me a photo. | もらう — I receive the favour |

---

### Sentence Structures at a Glance

| Structure | Who does the action | Particle for person | Example |
|-----------|---------------------|---------------------|---------|
| (speaker)**は** + (person)**に** + てあげる | speaker | に | 私は妹に教えてあげる |
| (person)**は** + (私)**に** + てくれる | someone else | に | むすこは私にかいてくれる |
| (speaker)**は** + (person)**に** + てもらう | someone else (for speaker) | に | 私はむすめに手伝ってもらう |

### Combined Sentences — あげる ＋ くれる Together

Two giving/receiving verbs can appear in the **same sentence**, connected by から (because):

**Example 1 — Grandpa → me → family:**
- そ父は私にたくさんくだ物を買って**くれました**から、私は家族にケーキを作って**あげました**。  
  *(Sofu wa watashi ni takusan kudamono wo katte kuremashita kara, watashi wa kazoku ni keeki wo tsukutte agemashita.)*  
  "Because my grandpa bought me a lot of fruit, I made a cake for my family."

  > "Grandpa" did "me" a favour (**くれる**), and "I" did a favour for "my family" (**あげる**).

**Example 2 — me → friend → me:**
- 私は友だちの動画にコメントを書いて**あげました**。友だちは私のコメントに「いいね！」ボタンを押して**くれました**。  
  *(Watashi wa tomodachi no douga ni komento wo kaite agemashita. Tomodachi wa watashi no komento ni "ii ne!" botan wo oshite kuremashita.)*  
  "I wrote a comment on my friend's video. My friend 'liked' my comment."

  > "I" did a favour (**あげる**) for "my friend", and "my friend" did "me" a favour (**くれる**).

**Example 3 — brother → me → (my action back):**
- 兄は私に料理して**くれました**から、私はさらを洗って**あげました**。  
  *(Ani wa watashi ni ryouri shite kuremashita kara, watashi wa sara wo aratte agemashita.)*  
  "Because my older brother cooked for me, I washed the dishes."

  > 料理（りょうり）= cooking; 洗う（あらう）= to wash

---

### Identifying the Correct Form — Decision Guide

When choosing あげる / くれる / もらう, ask: **"Who is the speaker in relation to the favour?"**

1. **Am I doing the favour for someone else?** → **てあげる**  
   → 私は友だちに写真を見せて**あげます**。

2. **Is someone doing the favour for me?** → **てくれる**  
   → 友だちは私に写真を見せて**くれます**。

3. **Am I asking/getting someone to do a favour for me?** → **てもらう**  
   → 私は店の人にくつを持ってきて**もらいました**。

## 3. Learning Context

- **Grammar Focus:** Fluency practice combining all three favour forms — てあげる, てくれる, てもらう — in context-rich real-life situations.
- **Key Exercise:** Identifying which form fits from the perspective stated (who gives, who receives, who is the speaker).
- **Combined sentences:** から (because) links two favour verbs together naturally — common in daily Japanese storytelling.
- **Vocabulary Note:** 料理（りょうり）= cooking/food; 皿（さら）= dish/plate; 洗う（あらう）= to wash — common household vocabulary.
- **Social Media Context:** Social media actions use てあげる (I comment on their video) and てくれる (they like my comment) naturally.
