## 2. Grammar & Sentences

### あげる / もらう / くれる — Review

These three verbs are used when giving or receiving things (objects or actions):

| Verb | Meaning | Used When |
|------|---------|-----------|
| あげる | to give | speaker (or 3rd person) gives to someone else |
| もらう | to receive | speaker receives from someone |
| くれる | to give | someone gives **to the speaker** (receiver = speaker) |

**Object example:**
- 女の子はお父さんにケーキをあげます。*(Onna no ko wa otousan ni keeki wo agemasu.)* — "The girl will give her father a cake."

---

### て Form ＋ あげる — Doing a Favour for Someone

When what is given is an **action** (not a thing), attach **あげる** after the **て form** of the action verb.

**Structure:** `verb て form` + `あげます / あげました`

**Meaning:** "(I/someone) does [action] as a favour for someone else."

**Comparison:**
| Japanese | English |
|----------|---------|
| ゆっくり話します。 | I speak slowly. |
| ゆっくり話してあげます。 | I speak slowly (for you). |

---

---

### Particles with てあげる — Key Rule

The particle used with the person (recipient) depends on the **base verb**:

| Verb | Particle for Person | Example |
|------|---------------------|---------|
| 貸す, 見せる, 教える | **に** (indirect object) | 友だち**に**本を貸してあげます。*(I'll lend a book to my friend.)* |
| 手伝う, つれていく | **を** (direct object) | 友だち**を**手伝ってあげます。*(I'll help my friend.)* |
| つれていく | **を** + destination **に** | 友だち**を**海**に**つれていってあげます。*(I'll take my friend to the sea.)* |

> **Why を for 手伝う and つれていく?** These verbs treat the person as the **direct object** of the action — you are acting on them directly, not giving something to them indirectly.

## 3. Learning Context

- **Grammar Focus:** て form ＋ あげる to describe doing an action as a favour for someone — extending the あげる/もらう/くれる giving/receiving system from objects to actions.
- **Key Pattern:** てあげる = "(I) do [action] for you/them (as a favour)." The favour direction is always away from the speaker.
- **Particle Caution:** Most verbs use に for the recipient, but 手伝う and つれていく use を because the person is the direct object — a common error point.
- **みせる / 見せる:** New vocabulary introduced in this section. Example: パスポートを見せてください。*(Please show me your passport.)*
- **Context:** Everyday favour-giving scenarios — teaching siblings, lending items, showing photos, taking pets to the mountain.
- **Cultural Note:** In Japanese culture, explicitly framing actions as favours (てあげる) carries a nuance of generosity — overusing it can sound condescending. Native speakers often use softer forms in real conversation.
