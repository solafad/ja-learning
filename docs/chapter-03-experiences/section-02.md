## 2. Grammar & Sentences

### ながら — Extended Practice

Reinforcement of the core pattern: **Verb 1 (masu stem) + ながら + Verb 2**

- ポップコーンを食べながら映画を見ます。*(Poppukoon wo tabenagara eiga wo mimasu.)* — I eat popcorn while watching a film.
- まんがを読みながらコーヒーを飲みます。*(Manga wo yominagara koohii wo nomimasu.)* — While reading manga, she drinks coffee.
- 歌いながらそうじします。*(Utainagara souji shimasu.)* — We sing while we are cleaning.
- シャワーをあびながら歌うのが好きです。*(Shawaa wo abinagara utau no ga suki desu.)* — I like singing while having a shower.
- きのうは一日中ラジオを聞きながら勉強していました。*(Kinou wa ichinichijuu rajio wo kikinagara benkyou shite imashita.)* — I was studying while listening to the radio all day yesterday.

### ながら with とき — Contextual Simultaneous Action

とき (when) can set up the scenario, with ながら describing the two actions within it.

- 学校に行くとき、音楽を聞きながら歩きます。*(Gakkou ni iku toki, ongaku wo kikinagara arukimasu.)* — When I go to school, I walk while listening to music.

### ながら in Past Tense

ながら itself does not change — only the final verb inflects for tense.

- きのうテレビを見ながらしゅくだいをしました。*(Kinou terebi wo minagara shukudai wo shimashita.)* — Yesterday I did my homework while watching TV.
- しゅくだいはしましたか？ーはい、きのうテレビを見ながらしゅくだいをしました。*(Shukudai wa shimashita ka? — Hai, kinou terebi wo minagara shukudai wo shimashita.)* — Did you do your homework? — Yes, I did my homework yesterday while watching TV.

## 3. Learning Context

- **Tense lives in Verb 2:** ながら is invariant — past/present/progressive is expressed only by the ending of Verb 2.
- **Subject omission:** When the subject is clear from context, Japanese drops it — シャワーをあびながら歌うのが好きです has no stated subject.
- **のが好きです pattern:** Verb (plain) + のが好きです = "I like doing ~" — a natural combination with ながら.
