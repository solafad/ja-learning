## 2. Grammar & Sentences

### ながら — Two Actions at the Same Time

**Structure:** `Verb 1 (masu stem) + ながら + Verb 2`

Remove ます from Verb 1, then attach ながら directly. The main action goes in Verb 2.

- 食べながら勉強します。*(Tabenagara benkyou shimasu.)* — While eating, I study.
- ドーナツを食べながらしゅくだいをします。*(Doonatsu wo tabenagara shukudai wo shimasu.)* — While eating doughnuts, I do my homework.
- テレビを見ながらポップコーンを食べます。*(Terebi wo minagara poppukoon wo tabemasu.)* — While watching TV, they eat popcorn.
- 音楽をききながら本を読みます。*(Ongaku wo kikinagara hon wo yomimasu.)* — While listening to music, she reads.
- 料理しながら歌います。*(Ryouri shinagara utaimasu.)* — While cooking, she sings.
- コーヒーを飲みながら彼女を待っています。*(Koohii wo nominagara kanojo wo matte imasu.)* — While waiting for my girlfriend, I am drinking coffee.
- 歩きながらアイスクリームを食べます。*(Aruki nagara aisukuriimu wo tabemasu.)* — While walking, he eats ice cream.
- 私は音楽をききながら宿題はできません。*(Watashi wa ongaku wo kikinagara shukudai wa dekimasen.)* — I cannot do my homework while listening to music.

### Polite Requests — てください / ないでください

Use these to ask someone to do or not to do something.

| Purpose | Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Please do ~ | て form + ください | 座って食べてください。*(Please sit and eat.)* |
| Please don't ~ | ない form + でください | 歩きながら食べないでください。*(Please do not eat while walking.)* |

- 歩きながら食べないでください。ここに座って食べてください。*(Aruki nagara tabenaide kudasai. Koko ni suwatte tabete kudasai.)* — Please do not eat while walking. Please sit down here and eat.
- 食べながら話さないでください。*(Tabenagara hanasanaide kudasai.)* — Please do not talk while eating.

### てはいけません — You May Not / Must Not

- 電話しながら運転してはいけません。車を止めてください。*(Denwa shinagara unten shite wa ikemasen. Kuruma wo tomete kudasai.)* — You may not drive while making a phone call. Please stop the car.

## 3. Learning Context

- **Core rule:** Before ながら, use the masu stem — remove ます entirely. Do NOT use the て form or the full masu form.
- **Subject omission:** In Japanese, the subject can be omitted when it is obvious from context — 音楽をききながら本を読みます can mean "she reads while listening to music" without stating who.
- **Cultural note:** Eating while walking (歩きながら) or on a local train (電車にのりながら) is often considered bad manners in Japan. Eating on a 新かんせん (shinkansen) is acceptable.
- **てはいけません** is a firm prohibition ("you must not"), stronger than ないでください (a polite request).
