## 2. Grammar & Sentences

### Verb Stem + 始める（はじめる）— "to start doing"

**Structure:** `verb stem` + `始めます / 始めました`

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| 毎日ジムに行き始めました。 | Mainichi jimu ni iki hajimemashita. | I've started going to the gym every day. |
| けんとさんとつき合い始めました。 | Kento-san to tsukiai hajimemashita. | I've started going out with Kento. |
| 人々は飛行機にのり始めました。 | Hitobito wa hikouki ni nori hajimemashita. | People started getting on the plane. |
| 飛行機は動き始めました。 | Hikouki wa ugoki hajimemashita. | The plane started moving. |
| となりの人は本を読み始めました。 | Tonari no hito wa hon wo yomi hajimemashita. | The person next to me started reading a book. |
| 空が明るくなり始めました。 | Sora ga akaruku nari hajimemashita. | The sky started getting brighter. |

---

### Verb Stem + 終（お）わる — "to finish doing"

**Structure:** `verb stem` + `終わります / 終わりました`

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| 私たちはまんがを読みおわりました。 | Watashitachi wa manga wo yomi owarimashita. | We've finished reading the manga. |
| 人々は座席に座り終わりました。 | Hitobito wa zaseki ni suwari owarimashita. | People have finished sitting in their seats. |
| 人々は機内食を食べおわりました。 | Hitobito wa kinai shoku wo tabe owarimashita. | People finished eating their in-flight meals. |
| パイロットは話し終わりました。飛行機は飛び始めました。 | Pairotto wa hanashi owarimashita. Hikouki wa tobi hajimemashita. | The pilot finished talking. The plane started flying. |

---

### Combining with たら — Sequential Actions

**Structure:** `verb stem + おわったら` = "when you finish doing…"

Used to make requests or describe what happens **after** completing an action.

- ペンを使いおわったら、私に貸してください。*(Pen wo tsukaiowattara, watashi ni kashite kudasai.)* — "If you've finished using the pen, please lend it to me."
- このお酒を飲みおわったら、赤ワインをおねがいします。*(Kono osake wo nomiowattara, aka wain wo onegaishimasu.)* — "When I finish this alcoholic drink, could I have a red wine, please?"
- ヘッドホンを使いおわったら、ここに入れてください。*(Heddohon wo tsukaiowattara, koko ni irete kudasai.)* — "When you finish using the headphones, please put them here."

---

### Combining with と — Natural Sequence

**Structure:** `verb stem + おわると` = "when (someone) finishes…, [automatic result]"

- パイロットが話し終わると、飛行機は止まりました。*(Pairotto ga hanashi owaru to, hikouki wa tomarimashita.)* — "When the pilot finished speaking, the plane stopped." *(The pilot finished speaking first.)*

---

### 〜た後で（あとで）— "After doing"

**Structure:** `plain past form` + `後で`

- 映画を見おわった後で、音楽を聞きました。*(Eiga wo miowatta ato de, ongaku wo kikimashita.)* — "After finishing watching a film, I listened to music."

---

### Kanji Note: 機内食（きないしょく）

| Kanji | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 機 | from 飛行機 (airplane) |
| 内 | inside |
| 食 | to eat |

Combined: 機内食 = "eating inside the plane" = **in-flight meal**

> Tip: Knowing individual kanji meanings can help you guess the meaning of unfamiliar compound words!

## 3. Learning Context

- **Grammar Focus:** Verb stem + 始める (start doing) and verb stem + 終わる (finish doing) — two highly productive patterns attached to any verb stem.
- **Combinations:** Both patterns work with たら (requests/sequences) and と (automatic consequences), and with 〜た後で (after doing).
- **Context:** All examples set on a flight to Japan — boarding, meals, headphones, in-flight movies, and arrival.
- **Cultural Note:** Japanese flight attendants use very polite forms of these expressions when making in-cabin announcements and requests.
