## 2. Grammar & Sentences

### Japanese Marks: O and X

| Mark | Reading | Meaning |
|------|---------|---------|
| O（まる） | maru | correct |
| X（ばつ） | batsu | incorrect |

---

### たら vs と — Key Differences

**Rule 1:** と can't be followed by a request (〜てください), an invitation (〜ませんか), or a suggestion (どうですか). The result after と must always happen automatically.

| | Example | Translation |
|-|---------|-------------|
| O | あつかったら、このボタンを押してください。| If it's hot, please press this button. |
| X | ~~あついと、このボタンを押してください。~~ | — |
| O | 雨がやんだら、バーベキューをしませんか。| If it stops raining, would you like to have a barbecue? |
| X | ~~雨がやむと、バーベキューをしませんか。~~ | — |

**Rule 2:** と can't be used for imaginary or unreal situations. Use たら instead.

| | Example | Translation |
|-|---------|-------------|
| O | 私はとりだったら、空を飛びます。| If I were a bird, I would fly in the sky. |
| X | ~~私はとりだと、空を飛びます。~~ | — |
| O | 私は犬だったら、雨の日に外へ行きません。| If I were a dog, I wouldn't go outside on rainy days. |
| X | ~~私は犬だと、雨の日に外へ行きません。~~ | — |

---

### Describing Hopes: 〜と、いいです

**Structure:** `casual form` + `と、いいです`  
**Meaning:** "I hope…" / "If…, it would be nice."

- **Key Phrase:** 本があると、いいです。*(Hon ga aru to, ii desu.)* — "If I have a book, it would be nice."

**Examples:**
- お金を入れると、ボタンが押せます。*(Okane wo ireru to, botan ga osemasu.)* — "If you insert some money, you can press the button."
- パイロットになると、色々な国に行けます。*(Pairotto ni naru to, iroiro na kuni ni ikemasu.)* — "If you become a pilot, you can go to various countries."
- アイマスクがあると、いいです。*(Aimasuku ga aru to, ii desu.)* — "I hope I have an eye mask."
- おもしろい映画があると、いいです。*(Omoshiroi eiga ga aru to, ii desu.)* — "I hope there are some interesting films."
- コーヒーが飲めると、いいですね。コーヒーが大好きなんです。*(Koohii ga nomeru to, ii desu ne. Koohii ga daisuki nan desu.)* — "If I could drink some coffee, it would be nice. I love coffee, actually."
- 飛行機がおくれないと、いいですが。*(Hikouki ga okurenai to, ii desu ga.)* — "I hope the flight won't be delayed but… (it might)."

---

### Particles: ね, よ, が with と、いいです

| Particle | Function | Example |
|----------|----------|---------|
| ね | seeking confirmation / agreement ("isn't it?; right?") | 明日は雨がふらないと、いいですね。*(I hope it doesn't rain tomorrow, don't you agree?)* |
| よ | speaker's conviction / assertion ("I'm telling you") | かさを持っていくと、いいですよ。*(If you bring an umbrella, it would be good, I'm telling you!)* |
| が | joins sentences to say "but" (second half can be omitted) | はれだと、いいですが（雨がふるかもしれません）。*(I hope it'll be sunny but (it might rain).)* |

**Note:** Although たら and と have different rules, either phrase can be used in と、いいです sentences to say "if."

## 3. Learning Context

- **Grammar Focus:** Contrast between たら and と — と requires automatic/inevitable results and cannot be used for requests, invitations, suggestions, or unreal/imaginary situations.
- **New Pattern:** 〜と、いいです expresses hopes about something happening.
- **Cultural Note:** O (まる) and X (ばつ) are the standard correct/incorrect marks used in Japanese schools and everyday life — O means correct, X means incorrect (opposite of many Western systems).
- **Context:** Lesson continues on a Japan-bound flight — practicing conditionals through in-flight scenarios.
