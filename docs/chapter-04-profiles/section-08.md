## 2. Kanji Breakdown

### 発 — Departure / Beginning

| Form       | Reading      | Meaning         |
|------------|--------------|-----------------|
| 発明する    | はつめいする  | To invent       |
| 出発する    | しゅっぱつする| To depart       |
| 発音        | はつおん      | Pronunciation   |
| 発見する    | はっけんする  | To discover     |

The radical of 発 is 癶, which describes the movement of legs. 癶 looks like a tent — when we take down a tent, we are ready to depart (出発する).

### 付 — With / To Attach

| Form      | Reading   | Meaning                    |
|-----------|-----------|----------------------------|
| 付き       | つき      | With (attached feature)    |
| 日付       | ひづけ    | Date                       |
| 付き合う   | つきあう  | To be in a relationship    |
| 受付       | うけつけ  | Reception; acceptance      |

The radical of 付 is イ (person). 休 and 何 share the same radical. Note: 付 has a dot in the middle — don't omit it.

### 欲 — Longing / Desire

| Form    | Reading    | Meaning   |
|---------|------------|-----------|
| 欲しい   | ほしい     | To want   |
| 食欲     | しょくよく | Appetite  |

The radical of 欲 is 欠 (open mouth). We open our mouths (欠) when we have a cavity (谷) in our stomach because we have an appetite (食欲). Also appears in 歌 (to sing) and 飲 (to drink).

### 集 — To Collect / To Gather

| Form    | Reading      | Meaning              |
|---------|--------------|----------------------|
| 集める   | あつめる     | To collect           |
| 集まる   | あつまる     | To gather            |
| 集中     | しゅうちゅう | Concentration        |
| 集金     | しゅうきん   | Money collection     |

The radical of 集 is 隹 (bird). Birds (隹) are gathering (集) on a tree (木).

### 始 — To Start

| Form     | Reading      | Meaning                       |
|----------|--------------|-------------------------------|
| 始める    | はじめる     | To start (something)          |
| 始まる    | はじまる     | (Something) starts            |
| 開始      | かいし       | Commencement                  |
| 始業式    | しぎょうしき | First day school assembly     |

The radical of 始 is 女 (woman).

## 3. Grammar & Sentences

- 日本へ出発する。*(Nihon e shuppatsu suru.)* — I'll depart for Japan.
- 付き合う。*(Tsukiau.)* — To be in a relationship.
- ちゅう車場付きの家。*(Chuushajou tsuki no ie.)* — A house with a car park.
- 今日はあまり食欲がありません。*(Kyou wa amari shokuyoku ga arimasen.)* — I don't have much of an appetite today.
- 鳥が木に集まっています。*(Tori ga ki ni atsumatte imasu.)* — The birds are gathering on the tree.
- 古いお金を集める。*(Furui okane wo atsumeru.)* — To collect old money.
- 仕事を始める。*(Shigoto wo hajimeru.)* — To start a job.
- よやくのうけ付を開始する。*(Yoyaku no uketsuke wo kaishi suru.)* — To start accepting reservations.
- 日本の切手を集めています。きょ年始めました。*(Nihon no kitte wo atsumete imasu. Kyonen hajimemashita.)* — I'm collecting Japanese postal stamps. I started last year.

## 4. Learning Context

- **出発 word order:** 出 comes before 発 — 出発する (shuppatsu), not 発出する. Common error in exercises: writing the kanji in reverse order.
- **食欲 word order:** 食 before 欲 — 食欲 (appetite = food + desire). Reversed 欲食 is wrong.
- **付 dot:** The kanji 付 has a small dot (丶) in the upper-right area — a common stroke to omit. 受付 (reception) is a useful anchor word.
- **始める vs 始まる:** 始める (transitive — I start something) vs 始まる (intransitive — something starts on its own). Same split as 集める (collect) vs 集まる (gather/come together).
- **始業式 cultural note:** In Japan, each school term starts with a special ceremony called 始業式 (しぎょうしき). The head of school gives a speech and pupils sing the school song. Japanese schools have 3 terms: April, September, and January.
- **発 compounds:** All four compounds use the はつ/ぱつ reading — 発明 (invention), 出発 (departure), 発音 (pronunciation), 発見 (discovery). The ぱつ reading appears in compounds where the preceding sound causes voicing (出発 → しゅっぱつ).
