## 2. Kanji Breakdown

### 猫 — Cat

| Form   | Reading | Meaning |
|--------|---------|---------|
| 猫      | ねこ    | Cat     |

猫 combines 犬 (animal radical 犭) with 苗 (seedling) — a creature that prowls the fields.

### 馬 — Horse

| Form   | Reading | Meaning |
|--------|---------|---------|
| 馬      | うま    | Horse   |

The strokes of 馬 trace a horse's mane and legs. The four bottom strokes represent four hooves.

### 鳥 — Bird

| Form   | Reading | Meaning |
|--------|---------|---------|
| 鳥      | とり    | Bird    |

鳥 depicts a bird with a beak, body, and tail feathers. Compare with 島 (island) — 鳥 has an extra horizontal stroke for the tail.

### 牛 — Cattle

| Form    | Reading      | Meaning          |
|---------|--------------|------------------|
| 牛       | うし         | Cattle           |
| 牛肉     | ぎゅうにく   | Beef             |
| 牛乳     | ぎゅうにゅう | Milk             |
| 和牛     | わぎゅう     | Wagyu / Japanese beef |

牛 shows a cow's head with horns. The on-reading ぎゅう appears in all food/product compounds.

### 魚 — Fish

| Form   | Reading    | Meaning   |
|--------|------------|-----------|
| 魚      | さかな     | Fish      |
| 金魚    | きんぎょ   | Goldfish  |
| 人魚    | にんぎょ   | Mermaid   |

魚 depicts a fish — head at top, body in the middle, tail fins at the bottom. The on-reading ぎょ appears in compounds (金魚, 人魚).

## 3. Grammar & Sentences

- 猫がいます。*(Neko ga imasu.)* — There is a cat.
- さくらさんの猫はかわいいです。*(Sakura-san no neko wa kawaii desu.)* — Sakura's cat is cute.
- 馬に乗ります。*(Uma ni norimasu.)* — I'll ride a horse.
- 鳥がとんでいます。*(Tori ga tonde imasu.)* — Birds are flying.
- にわに鳥がたくさんいます。*(Niwa ni tori ga takusan imasu.)* — There are many birds in the garden.
- 牛乳を飲みます。*(Gyuunyuu wo nomimasu.)* — I'll drink some milk.
- かずやくんは魚と牛肉が大好きです。*(Kazuya-kun wa sakana to gyuuniku ga daisuki desu.)* — Kazuya likes fish and beef very much.

## 4. Learning Context

- **牛 kun vs on readings:** うし is used for the animal itself; ぎゅう appears in all compounds (牛肉, 牛乳, 和牛). A safe rule: if 牛 is combined with another kanji, read it ぎゅう.
- **魚 kun vs on readings:** さかな is the standalone word for fish; ぎょ appears in compounds (金魚 = gold + fish, 人魚 = person + fish = mermaid).
- **鳥 vs 島:** These look similar. 鳥 (bird) has one more horizontal stroke than 島 (island). Mnemonic: a bird needs an extra stroke to take flight.
- **Animal habitats:** 魚 → underwater (水の中), 鳥 → sky/trees (空・木), 牛 → farm (農場). Useful for いる/います location sentences.
- **乗る + に:** The particle for riding is に — 馬に乗る, 電車に乗る, 自転車に乗る. The vehicle/animal takes に, not を.
