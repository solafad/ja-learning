## 2. Grammar & Sentences

### そう (Casual) — Looks / Seems Like

Drop the です from formal そうです to get casual そう. Attach directly to an i-adjective stem (drop い) or na-adjective.

**Structure:** `[adj stem] + そう`

| Adjective   | Casual そう form  | Meaning                  |
|-------------|------------------|--------------------------|
| 悲しい       | 悲しそう          | Looks sad                |
| さびしい     | さびしそう        | Looks lonely             |
| 楽しい       | 楽しそう          | Looks fun                |
| つまらない   | つまらなそう      | Looks boring             |
| はずかしい   | はずかしそう      | Looks embarrassed        |
| くやしい     | くやしそう        | Looks frustrated         |
| 幸せ（な）   | 幸せそう          | Looks happy              |

- みきさんは悲しそう。*(Miki-san wa kanashisou.)* — Miki looks sad.
- あの猫はさびしそう。*(Ano neko wa sabishisou.)* — That cat looks lonely.
- 友だちとあそんでいて楽しそう。*(Tomodachi to asonde ite tanoshisou.)* — He is playing with his friends, and it looks fun.
- つまらなそうだね。いっしょにゲームをしない？*(Tsumaranasou da ne. Issho ni geemu wo shinai?)* — You look bored. Why don't we play a game together?
- あきとくんに話すとき、はずかしそう。*(Akito-kun ni hanasu toki, hazukashisou.)* — When you talk to Akito, you look embarrassed.
- あいさんはテストができなかったと言ってた。とてもくやしそうだった。*(Ai-san wa tesuto ga dekinakatta to itteta. Totemo kuyashisou datta.)* — Ai said she did not do well on the test. She looked very frustrated.
- つまが幸せそうだから私も幸せだ。*(Tsuma ga shiawasesou dakara watashi mo shiawase da.)* — Because my wife looks happy, I am happy, too.

### Casual Passive — Plain Form

The passive voice in casual speech uses the plain (dictionary-register) passive form. The key marker is dropping い from て-いる contractions.

**Passive formation:**

| Verb type      | Dictionary | Passive (plain) |
|----------------|------------|-----------------|
| Group 1 (う)   | 書く       | 書かれる         |
| Group 1 (う)   | 建てる → Group 2 | 建てられる  |
| Group 2 (る)   | 作る → Group 1 | 作られる       |
| Irregular      | する       | される           |

**Casual contractions:**
- 書かれている → 書かれてる *(casual: drop the い)*
- 知っていた → 知ってた

**Passive sentences:**

- この新聞は日本語で書かれている。*(Kono shinbun wa Nihongo de kakarete iru.)* — This newspaper is written in Japanese.
- チーズは牛にゅうから作られる。*(Chiizu wa gyuunyuu kara tsukuraru.)* — Cheese is made from milk.
- 来年ここにビルが建てられる。*(Rainen koko ni biru ga taterareru.)* — A building will be built here next year.
- このえは今から100年くらい前にかかれた。*(Kono e wa ima kara hyakunen kurai mae ni kakareta.)* — This painting was painted around 100 years ago.
- え文字は日本で作られた。知ってた？*(Emoji wa Nihon de tsukureta. Shitteta?)* — Emoji were made in Japan. Did you know?
- スカイツリーはいつ建てられた？*(Sukaitsuri wa itsu taterareta?)* — When was Skytree built?
- ここに何が書かれてる？よく分からない。*(Koko ni nani ga kakareteru? Yoku wakaranai.)* — What is written here? I don't really know.
- 日本ではどんなものが発明された？*(Nihon de wa donna mono ga hatsumei sareta?)* — What kind of things were invented in Japan?
- この家はお父さんが生まれる前に建てられたの？すごいね。*(Kono ie wa otousan ga umareru mae ni taterareta no? Sugoi ne.)* — Was this house built before my father was born? It's great, isn't it?

## 3. Learning Context

- **Casual そう vs formal そうです:** Remove です for casual register — 楽しそうです → 楽しそう. The adjective stem modification (drop い) is the same in both.
- **つまらなそう:** つまらない drops the い → つまらなそう, not つまらいそう or つまらそう. The な is retained.
- **Passive agent particle:** Use に for a person agent (先生にほめられた — praised by the teacher) and で for material/medium (日本語で書かれている — written in Japanese).
- **Dropped い in casual speech:** て-いる contractions: 書かれている → 書かれてる, 知っていた → 知ってた. This is standard in spoken Japanese and informal writing.
- **Sentence-final の:** の at the end of a question (建てられたの？) softens the tone — common in casual feminine or child speech, but used broadly in informal contexts.
- **知ってた？:** Past form of 知っている in casual register — "did you know?" Used as a rhetorical hook when sharing surprising facts (え文字は日本で作られた。知ってた？).
- **発明 in passive:** 発明された (was invented) — same 発 kanji from section-08. Passive される attaches to する-verb compounds.
