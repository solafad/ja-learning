## 2. Kanji Breakdown

### 宿 — Inn / House

| Form    | Reading  | Meaning     |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| 宿題     | しゅくだい | Homework    |
| 宿泊     | しゅくはく | Lodging     |

宿 can hold 100（百）people（イ）under its roof（宀）.

### 題 — Topic / Subject

| Form    | Reading  | Meaning              |
|---------|----------|----------------------|
| 宿題     | しゅくだい | Homework             |
| 問題     | もんだい  | Problem              |
| 話題     | わだい   | Topic of conversation |

The radical of 題 is 頁 ("leaf"). On the left: 日（daily）+ 正（correct）— you write correct answers daily in your homework（宿題）.

### 建 — Build

| Form     | Reading  | Meaning  |
|----------|----------|----------|
| 建てる    | たてる   | To build |
| 建物      | たてもの | Building |

The radical of 建 is 廴 ("long stride"). The right side has 5 horizontal strokes — imagine the floors of a building.

### 寺 — Buddhist Temple

| Form   | Reading      | Meaning          |
|--------|--------------|------------------|
| お寺    | おてら       | Buddhist temple  |
| 寺（suffix）| -でら / -じ | Temple (in names) |

The radical of 寺 is 寸 (an old unit = the width of one finger). When てら appears in a temple name, it often shifts to でら via rendaku.

**Famous temples:**

| Temple           | Reading          | Location |
|------------------|------------------|----------|
| 清水寺            | きよみずでら      | 京都      |
| 浅草寺            | せんそうじ        | 東京      |
| 金閣寺            | きんかくじ        | 京都      |
| 法隆寺            | ほうりゅうじ      | 奈良      |

法隆寺（ほうりゅうじ）was built in AD 607 — the oldest wooden building in the world. It has survived earthquakes of magnitude 7.0 or greater 46 times.

### 絵 — Picture / Painting

| Form    | Reading | Meaning               |
|---------|---------|-----------------------|
| 絵       | え      | Picture; drawing      |
| 絵文字   | えもじ  | Emoji                 |

When different colours of thread（糸）meet（会）, they make a beautiful picture（絵）.

## 3. Grammar & Sentences

- 先週金かく寺に行きました。*(Senshuu Kinkakuji ni ikimashita.)* — I went to Kinkaku-ji last week.
- かなさんは絵が上手です。*(Kana-san wa e ga jouzu desu.)* — Kana is good at painting.
- ほうりゅう寺は日本で一番古い建物です。*(Houryuuji wa Nihon de ichiban furui tatemono desu.)* — Houryu-ji is the oldest building in Japan.
- 今日の宿題は、お寺の絵をかくことです。*(Kyou no shukudai wa, otera no e wo kaku koto desu.)* — Today's homework is drawing a temple.
- おもしろい話題。*(Omoshiroi wadai.)* — Interesting topic.

## 4. Learning Context

- **宿 vs 題 compounds:** Both appear in 宿題 — 宿 contributes "lodging/place to stay" and 題 contributes "subject/topic." The compound literally means "a topic to work on at home."
- **建てる vs 建物:** 建てる (verb: to build) uses the kun-reading た. 建物 (noun: building) also uses た for 建 but もの for 物. The on-reading けん appears in formal contexts (建設 kensetsu — construction).
- **寺 rendaku:** てら in isolation → でら in compound temple names (清水寺 きよみずでら, not きよみずてら). The suffix -じ (on-reading) is used for shorter temple name suffixes (金閣寺 きんかくじ).
- **絵 mnemonic:** 糸（thread）+ 会（meet）= 絵（picture）. The 糸 radical signals colour/textile — threads woven together form a painting.
