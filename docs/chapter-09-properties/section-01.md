## 2. Japanese Apartment Size System

### LDK — Room Codes

Japanese properties use a coded system to describe layout:

| Code | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| L | Living room (リビング) |
| D | Dining room (ダイニング) |
| K | Kitchen (キッチン) |
| Number prefix | Number of separate rooms |

| Type | Layout | Size |
|------|--------|------|
| ワンルーム | Studio — everything in one space | Smallest — compact, cheap, popular with students and singles |
| 1LDK | 1 room + living room, dining room & kitchen | Medium |
| 2LDK | 2 rooms + living room, dining room & kitchen | Larger |

> **True/False:** "If there is a room, a living room, a dining room and a kitchen in an apartment, we call it a 1LDK." → **True**
> **Comparison:** 2LDKはワンルームより大きいです。*(Ni-eru-dī-kē wa wanrūmu yori ōkii desu.)* — "A 2LDK apartment is larger than a studio."

---

### 帖（じょう）— Measuring Rooms in Tatami Mats

Japanese people measure room area using the size of **tatami mats (たたみ)**.

- 1 tatami mat = **180cm long × 90cm wide ≈ 1.65m²**
- 六帖（ろくじょう）= 6 tatami mats ≈ **10m²**

| Expression | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 六帖 | ろくじょう | 6 tatami mats ≈ 10m² |
| 十帖 | じゅうじょう | 10 tatami mats ≈ 16.5m² |
| 十八帖 | じゅうはちじょう | 18 tatami mats ≈ 30m² |

- **Example:** 私の部屋は十じょうです。*(Watashi no heya wa jūjō desu.)* — "My room is the size of 10 tatami mats."
- **Example:** この部屋は六じょうで少しせまいですが、リビングは十八じょうあります。*(Kono heya wa rokujō de sukoshi semai desu ga, ribingu wa jūhachijō arimasu.)* — "This room is the size of 6 tatami mats, so it's a bit small, but the living room is the size of 18 tatami mats."

## 3. Property Listing Terms

### ペット可（か）— Pets Allowed

You'll see **ペット可（か）** in a property listing if pets are welcome.

- か／可 means **"allowed"**
- **Example:** ねこがいます。このアパートはペットかですか？*(Neko ga imasu. Kono apāto wa petto ka desu ka?)* — "I have a cat. Are pets allowed in this small apartment?"
- **Example:** このアパートはペット可ですね。犬がほしいです。*(Kono apāto wa petto ka desu ne. Inu ga hoshii desu.)* — "Pets are allowed in this small apartment, aren't they? I want a dog."

---

### ちゅうしゃじょう／駐車場 — Car Park

- **Example:** ちゅう車場に車をとめます。*(Chūshajō ni kuruma wo tomemasu.)* — "I park my car in the car park."
- **Example:** ちゅう車場つきの家をさがしています。*(Chūshajō-tsuki no ie wo sagashite imasu.)* — "I am looking for a house with a car park."
- **Example:** このマンションにはちゅう車場があります。でも月に二万円はらいます。*(Kono manshon ni wa chūshajō ga arimasu. Demo tsuki ni niman en haraimasu.)* — "This multi-storey apartment has a car park. But you pay 20,000 yen per month for it."

> **Note:** 月に (*tsuki ni*) means "per month".

---

### おきば／置き場 — Storage Space

- **Used in compounds:** 自転車置き場（じてんしゃおきば）= bicycle storage
- **Example:** 自転車は、自転車おき場にとめてください。*(Jitensha wa, jitensha okiba ni tomete kudasai.)* — "Please park your bicycle in the bicycle storage."
- **Example:** 自転車で学校に行くので、自転車おき場があると便利です。*(Jitensha de gakkō ni iku node, jitensha okiba ga aru to benri desu.)* — "Because I go to school by bicycle, it would be convenient if there was bicycle storage."

> **Grammar note:** と in 「あると便利です」is used to make an **"if"** sentence — "if there is X, it is convenient."

> **Culture note:** Cars and bicycles should be parked in designated areas, as roads around residential areas often have restricted parking.

## 6. Learning Context

- **Topic:** Reading Japanese property listings — apartment types, room sizes, and listing codes
- **Culture Note:** Room area in Japan is measured in **帖（じょう）** — tatami mat units. 六帖（ろくじょう）≈ 10m². Property listings also use codes like ペット可, 駐車場つき, and 自転車置き場 to describe features.
- **Grammar Focus:** LDK room codes; 帖（じょう）for size; つきの for "with a (feature)"; と for "if" in 〜があると便利
